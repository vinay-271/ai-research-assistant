## Company overview
Kotak Mahindra Bank Limited is one of India's leading private sector banks, originating as a financial services firm in 1985 before receiving a commercial banking license from the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) in 2003. This transition marked the first time in Indian banking history that a non-banking financial company (NBFC) was converted into a full-scale commercial bank. Headquartered in Mumbai, Maharashtra, the bank serves over 52 million customers across its network of over 2,200 branches. Under the leadership of Managing Director & CEO Ashok Vaswani, and with founder Uday Kotak active as a non-executive director, the bank operates a diversified financial services conglomerate with business lines across commercial lending, investment banking, asset management, life insurance, and retail broking.

## Business segments
*   **Consumer / Retail Banking:** Offers customized liability and asset products to retail customers, including savings accounts, current accounts, personal loans, home mortgages, credit cards, microfinance, and digital banking services.
*   **Corporate & Wholesale Banking:** Delivers working capital, term loans, project finance, trade services, structured debt syndication, and cash management solutions to large Indian conglomerates and multinational entities.
*   **Commercial Banking:** Services the agricultural sector, commercial vehicle owners, healthcare institutions, and medium/small enterprise (MSME) clients through highly specialized regional credit underwriting.
*   **Capital Markets & Advisory:** Provides investment banking, corporate advisory, and equity underwriting solutions through Kotak Mahindra Capital, along with retail and institutional broking through Kotak Securities.
*   **Asset Management and Insurance:** Comprises the mutual fund operations managed by Kotak AMC, alternative asset investments, and life insurance products offered through Kotak Mahindra Life Insurance.

## Products
*   **Retail Liability Accounts:** Savings accounts, salary accounts, high-yield current accounts, and specialized digital onboarding accounts under the "Kotak 811" program.
*   **Consumer Credit Portfolio:** Mortgages, home construction loans, auto loans, unsecured personal loans, gold loans, and credit cards.
*   **Commercial Credit Products:** Agricultural tractor financing, medical equipment loans, commercial vehicle financing, and working capital lines for MSMEs.
*   **Advisory and Capital Solutions:** Equity and debt capital markets underwriting, mergers and acquisitions advisory, institutional research, and customized wealth management packages for ultra-high-net-worth clients (Kotak Cherry).

## Financial highlights
Consolidated and standalone audited metrics for the fiscal year ended March 31, 2026, show steady operating parameters:
*   **Standalone Net Profit (PAT):** Reached ₹14,008 Crore, up 2.0% year-on-year compared to ₹13,720 Crore in FY25 (excluding one-time divestment gains).
*   **Consolidated Net Profit (PAT):** Stood at ₹19,288 Crore (or ₹19,103 Crore excluding divestment gains on Zurich Kotak General Insurance and Infina), growing 6.0% year-on-year.
*   **Net Interest Income (NII):** Annual standalone NII rose 6.0% year-on-year to ₹30,010 Crore.
*   **Net Interest Margin (NIM):** Standalone NIM was 4.60% for the full year, representing a gradual contraction from 4.96% in the previous fiscal year.
*   **Balance Sheet Growth:** Standalone deposits grew 15.0% year-on-year to ₹5.72 Lakh Crore, while net advances grew 16.0% to ₹4.96 Lakh Crore.
*   **CASA Ratio:** Maintained a strong customer liability profile with a Current Account Savings Account (CASA) ratio of 43.3% as of March 31, 2026.
*   **Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR):** Stood at a strong 23.0%, with a Common Equity Tier 1 (CET-1) ratio of 22.1%, signaling substantial excess capital.
*   **Asset Quality:** Standalone Gross NPA improved to 1.20% (down from 1.30% in Q3 FY26), and Net NPA stood at 0.25%, with a provision coverage ratio of 79%.

## Competitive advantages
*   **Strong Capital Position:** With a CAR of 23.0%, the bank has significant excess capital buffers, enabling it to pursue selective inorganic acquisitions, loan portfolios, and expansion without near-term capital dilution.
*   **Lower Funding Cost:** The CASA ratio of 43.3% remains structurally higher than many private peers, which helps keep the cost of deposit funding lower and supports net interest margins.
*   **Integrated Group Model:** Subsidiaries contribute approximately 23% of consolidated profits, allowing for comprehensive cross-selling of capital market products, insurance, and asset management to the core banking client base.
*   **Prudent Underwriting and Risk Culture:** Historically maintains clean asset quality and low credit costs, supported by conservative credit evaluation processes and early risk warning triggers.

## Risk factors
*   **NIM Compression:** High competitive intensity for deposits and the repricing of existing term deposits could further compress Net Interest Margins over the medium term.
*   **Structural Disintermediation:** A structural consumer shift of household savings away from traditional bank deposits into higher-yielding mutual funds and equities increases competition for retail liabilities.
*   **Technology & IT Security Risks:** The rapid scaling of digital onboarding post-RBI restriction lift increases transaction volumes, requiring continuous investments in IT systems to ensure technical stability and cybersecurity.
*   **Integration and Regulatory Complexity:** Departmentalizing operations from previously distinct subsidiaries (such as absorbing the lending business of Kotak Mahindra Investments Limited) poses operational and regulatory integration risks.

## Management outlook
Under the leadership of CEO Ashok Vaswani, the bank's strategy is focused on deploying its surplus capital to drive growth through both organic expansion and inorganic acquisitions. The bank is positioning itself as a "younger, leaner, and hungrier" organization, emphasizing digital acceleration and platform efficiencies. While management expects Net Interest Margins to face gradual downward pressure in FY27 due to deposit repricing, the focus remains on keeping a balanced credit-to-deposit growth ratio. The bank also plans to continue expanding alternative asset portfolios and non-banking businesses where sustainable return profiles are on offer.
