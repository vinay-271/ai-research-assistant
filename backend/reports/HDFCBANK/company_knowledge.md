## Company overview
HDFC Bank Limited is India's largest private sector bank by assets and a globally recognized financial services conglomerate. Established in 1994, the bank completed a historic merger with its parent housing finance entity, Housing Development Finance Corporation (HDFC) Limited, in July 2023. This consolidation created a massive financial services provider with a balance sheet crossing ₹43.6 lakh crore. Headquartered in Mumbai, Maharashtra, the bank operates an extensive distribution network of over 9,000 branches serving retail, corporate, and small business segments. Managing Director and CEO Sashidhar Jagdishan leads the bank's operations. In March 2026, Keki Mistry was appointed interim part-time chairman following the sudden resignation of Atanu Chakraborty.

## Business segments
*   **Retail Banking:** The largest business unit of the bank, offering a comprehensive suite of liability products, personal loans, home mortgages, credit cards, auto loans, and customized digital transaction services to individual consumers and small business merchants.
*   **Wholesale & Corporate Banking:** Caters to large corporate houses, multinational companies, public sector undertakings, and middle-market enterprises, providing project finance, working capital, trade services, structured debt, and syndication services.
*   **Treasury Operations:** Manages the bank's investment portfolios, regulatory liquidity reserves (such as SLR and CRR), foreign exchange trading, asset-liability matching, and derivative hedging products.
*   **Other Financial Services (Subsidiaries):** Operates across insurance, asset management, and stock broking through market-leading subsidiaries such as HDFC Life, HDFC ERGO, HDFC Asset Management Company (AMC), and HDFC Securities.

## Products
*   **Retail Liability Products:** Tailored savings accounts, salary accounts, high-yield current accounts, and specialized recurring/fixed deposits.
*   **Secured and Unsecured Retail Credit:** Home loans (mortgages), auto loans, commercial vehicle finance, gold loans, personal loans, and a leading credit card portfolio.
*   **Corporate & Commercial Banking Solutions:** Working capital facilities, corporate overdrafts, export-import trade credit, letter of credit, cash management services, and customized supply chain finance.
*   **Digital Platforms:** Modern consumer apps, including the mobile banking suite, PayZapp (payments and lifestyle ecosystem), and HDFC Bank Vyapaar (merchant onboarding and transaction management app).

## Financial highlights
Consolidated and standalone financial parameters for the fiscal year ended March 31, 2026, indicate a stable trajectory:
*   **Consolidated Balance Sheet:** Total assets expanded to ₹43.65 Lakh Crore (₹43,649 billion) as of March 31, 2026, compared to ₹39.10 Lakh Crore (₹39,102 billion) in the previous fiscal year.
*   **Standalone Deposits:** Rose 14.4% year-on-year to ₹31.05 Lakh Crore (₹31,053 billion), driven by retail savings and targeted term deposit mobilization.
*   **Standalone Advances:** Reached ₹29.37 Lakh Crore (₹29,372 billion), up 10.0% year-on-year, showing a measured credit strategy.
*   **Standalone Net Interest Margin (NIM):** Stood at 3.38% on total assets (3.53% on interest-earning assets).
*   **Capital Adequacy Ratio:** Maintained at a comfortable 19.7%, with a Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) ratio of 17.3%.
*   **Asset Quality:** Standalone Gross NPA (GNPA) was 1.20% (with ex-agri GNPA at 0.91%), and Net NPA (NNPA) was 0.33%, reflecting strong risk management practices.

## Competitive advantages
*   **Mega-Conglomerate Scale:** The post-merger balance sheet size enables HDFC Bank to participate in major corporate credit consortia, infrastructure project financing, and institutional banking mandates.
*   **Unrivaled Cross-Selling Opportunities:** The HDFC Ltd integration provides direct access to millions of high-quality mortgage customers, allowing the bank to cross-sell retail liability products, wealth advisory, and general/life insurance.
*   **Granular Branch Footprint:** A vast branch network, with more than 50% located in rural and semi-urban locations, establishes deep liability gathering capabilities and low-cost deposit mobilization.
*   **Pristine Asset Quality:** Continues to maintain some of the lowest non-performing asset (NPA) ratios among major global and domestic banks, driven by conservative retail credit policies.

## Risk factors
*   **Governance and Board Instability:** The sudden resignation of Part-Time Chairman Atanu Chakraborty in March 2026 over ethical and values congruence has introduced boardroom uncertainty.
*   **Succession Challenges:** With MD & CEO Sashidhar Jagdishan's initial term expiring in October 2026, regulatory focus remains on establishing a permanent chairman before addressing executive term renewals.
*   **Post-Merger Funding Pressures:** A high Loan-to-Deposit Ratio (LDR) requires the bank to continuously mobilize large volumes of low-cost retail deposits to phase out high-cost wholesale liabilities.
*   **Deposit Pricing Pressures:** Intense competition for retail CASA (Current Account Savings Account) deposits across the banking sector poses near-term margin pressure on interest spreads.

## Management outlook
The management team is focused on navigating the bank's post-stabilization phase. Rather than chasing aggressive, high-risk loan growth, the priority is rebalancing the Loan-to-Deposit Ratio (LDR). Management expects loans and deposits to compound at 12% and 13% annually over the FY26-28 period, requiring the mobilization of ₹4 to ₹4.5 lakh crore in fresh deposits. To support this, the bank is optimizing operating expenses (holding the core cost-to-income ratio around 39.9%) and using its digital platforms to lower acquisition costs. Additionally, the board is working with regulators to identify and appoint a permanent chairman.
