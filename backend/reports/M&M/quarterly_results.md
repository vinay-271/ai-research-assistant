## Revenue
For the fourth quarter (Q4) of the fiscal year ended March 31, 2026, Mahindra & Mahindra reported a consolidated revenue from operations of ₹54,982 Crore. This represents a 29.0% year-on-year growth compared to the ₹42,599 Crore reported in Q4 FY25, driven by volume growth in the automotive segment and steady performance in industrial services.

## PAT
The company reported a consolidated Profit After Tax (PAT) of ₹4,668 Crore for Q4 FY26, representing a 42.0% increase compared to ₹3,295 Crore in the corresponding quarter of the previous fiscal year. Standalone net profit for the quarter stood at ₹3,737.27 Crore, up 53% year-on-year from ₹2,437 Crore, supported by operating leverage and favorable raw material costs.

## EPS
For the quarter ended March 31, 2026, standalone basic Earnings Per Share (EPS) stood at ₹30.05, while consolidated basic EPS for the quarter was reported at ₹37.60.

## Key operational highlights
*   **Record Auto Volumes:** Overall automotive sales volumes grew 21% year-on-year to 307,000 units in Q4 FY26 (including Last Mile Mobility and electric vehicles), driven by utility vehicle demand.
*   **SUV Leadership:** M&M ended the quarter with a 24.5% SUV revenue market share, supported by stable monthly off-take of the Scorpio-N, XUV700, and Thar.
*   **Tractor Market Share:** Tractor market share rose by 30 basis points to 43.6% for the full year, helping offset a relatively flat domestic tractor industry.
*   **Last Mile Mobility:** Solidified its top spot in electric three-wheelers, maintaining a 40.0% segment market share through the Treo and Zor Grand models.
*   **Subsidiary Progress:** Tech Mahindra reported an EBIT margin improvement of 290 basis points to 12.6%, while Mahindra Finance recorded a 12% growth in Assets Under Management (AUM).
*   **Growth Gems Momentum:** M&M's growth portfolio companies, including real estate, logistics, and Accelo, reported a combined profit growth of 50.0% for the quarter.

## Management commentary
Dr. Anish Shah, Group CEO & Managing Director, commented on the quarter's results:

"FY26 has been a defining year marked by strong execution and breakthrough performance across several Group companies, despite geopolitical headwinds and several disruptions throughout the year. Our Auto and Farm segments further strengthened their market leadership, supported by sustained customer demand for our differentiated products and operational discipline. 

Tech Mahindra reported healthy traction in key new deal wins and operating margin improvements despite a challenging global spending environment, while Mahindra Finance pivoted back to growth on the back of stable asset quality. Our 'Growth Gems' have demonstrated significant momentum, and the Group is well poised to accelerate in these uncertain times."

## Future guidance
*   **Automotive Capacity Expansion:** The company is on track to expand its SUV production capacity to 49,000 units per month by the second quarter of the next fiscal year, aimed at resolving outstanding customer backlogs.
*   **Born Electric (EV) Timeline:** Management confirmed that the first model of its new "Born Electric" SUV series, the BE.05, is scheduled for its commercial launch by late 2026, supported by ongoing capital infusion.
*   **Return Metrics:** M&M maintains its target to deliver a minimum Return on Equity (RoE) of 18% across its key operating companies through disciplined capital allocation.
*   **Agricultural Equipment:** The company plans to scale its farm machinery business, introducing localized implements and exporting lightweight OJA tractors to North American and Southeast Asian markets.
