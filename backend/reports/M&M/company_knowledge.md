## Company overview
Mahindra & Mahindra (M&M) Limited, established in 1945 as a steel trading company, is one of India's largest multinational conglomerates and the flagship company of the Mahindra Group. Headquartered in Mumbai, Maharashtra, the company has grown into a leading player across utility vehicles, commercial vehicles, and agricultural tractors globally. M&M is the world's largest tractor manufacturer by volume and India's leading sport utility vehicle (SUV) manufacturer by revenue market share. Led by Group CEO & Managing Director Dr. Anish Shah, M&M operates across a diversified business ecosystem including financial services, information technology, real estate, hospitality, and logistics.

## Business segments
*   **Automotive Segment:** Focuses on the design, manufacturing, and marketing of SUVs, multi-purpose vehicles (MPVs), light commercial vehicles (LCVs) under 3.5 tonnes, medium and heavy commercial vehicles (M&HCVs), and electric three-wheelers.
*   **Farm Equipment Segment:** Comprises agricultural tractors, power tillers, harvesters, and specialized farm machinery designed for both domestic and international markets.
*   **Financial Services:** Operated through its listed subsidiary, Mahindra & Mahindra Financial Services Limited (MMFSL), providing retail financing for vehicles, tractors, housing, and small businesses in rural and semi-urban markets.
*   **Information Technology:** Driven by its listed subsidiary, Tech Mahindra Limited, providing digital transformation, consulting, and business process services globally.
*   **Growth Gems (Growth Portfolio):** Comprises high-momentum businesses across specialized sectors, including real estate (Mahindra Lifespaces), supply chain logistics (Mahindra Logistics), sustainable hospitality (Club Mahindra), and recycling (Mahindra Accelo).

## Products
*   **Sport Utility Vehicles (SUVs):** Highly popular domestic models including the Scorpio-N, XUV700, Thar, Bolero, XUV3XO, and the upcoming "Born Electric" (BE) SUV series.
*   **Light Commercial Vehicles (LCVs):** High-volume payload models such as the Bolero Maxitruck, Bolero Pik-Up, and Jeeto.
*   **Farm Tractors:** Specialized domestic and global tractor series under the Mahindra, Swaraj, and OJA brand platforms.
*   **Electric Vehicles (EVs):** Treo electric three-wheelers, Zor Grand cargo commercial vehicles, and the XUV400 passenger electric SUV.

## Financial highlights
Consolidated financial performance for the fiscal year ended March 31, 2026, shows robust growth and strict capital allocation discipline:
*   **Consolidated Revenue:** ₹1,98,639 Crore, representing a year-on-year growth of 25.0% compared to ₹1,59,211 Crore in FY25.
*   **Consolidated Profit After Tax (PAT):** ₹17,099 Crore, marking a 35.0% increase year-on-year (excluding one-time exceptional land sale gains of ₹304 Crore in FY25).
*   **Return on Equity (RoE):** Stood at 20.1% on a consolidated basis, reflecting capital efficiency.
*   **Consolidated EPS:** Reported at ₹152.2 for the full financial year.
*   **Dividend:** The Board recommended a final dividend of ₹33.00 per share (face value ₹5), marking a 30.0% increase over the previous year's payout.

## Competitive advantages
*   **Dominant SUV Revenue Market Share:** Holds the top spot in India's highly competitive SUV segment with a 25.3% revenue market share, driven by strong demand for core brands like Scorpio and XUV700.
*   **Unrivaled Agricultural Tractor Leadership:** Commands a 43.6% market share in the Indian tractor industry, benefiting from dual-brand distribution (Mahindra and Swaraj) and deep-rural dealership networks.
*   **Rural Finance Synergy:** Integration with Mahindra Financial Services provides the group with unique insights into rural customer credit profiles, supporting vehicle and tractor financing in semi-urban areas.
*   **Pioneering Electric Three-Wheeler Fleet:** Holds a dominant 40% market share in the electric three-wheeler cargo and passenger segments, positioning M&M as a key player in last-mile green logistics.

## Risk factors
*   **Monsoon Variability and Rural Income:** Farm segment demand is highly dependent on even monsoon distribution; uneven rainfall directly impacts rural incomes, crop yields, and subsequent tractor purchases.
*   **Supply Chain and Raw Material Vulnerability:** Subject to fluctuations in global commodity prices (steel, aluminum, and rubber) and dependencies on imported semiconductor components for advanced automotive electronics.
*   **Intense Electric Vehicle Competition:** Rapid technological evolution and aggressive pricing by domestic and global players in the passenger EV space require high capital investments in M&M's electric platforms.
*   **Geopolitical Headwinds:** Supply chain disruptions and high freight costs present ongoing challenges for the company's export-oriented auto and farm equipment divisions.

## Management outlook
M&M's management is focused on expanding manufacturing capacity across its high-demand SUV models to reduce customer wait times, aiming for a production capacity of 49,000 units per month. The strategic priority remains the launch and scaling of the "Born Electric" SUV portfolio, built on the modular INGLO platform. In the farm equipment segment, the company plans to introduce high-horsepower lightweight tractors on the OJA platform for export markets. The management team aims to sustain consolidated RoE above 18% through strict capital allocation and by scaling high-potential "Growth Gems" like logistics and real estate.
