## Revenue
For the fourth quarter (Q4) of the fiscal year ended March 31, 2026, Sun Pharmaceutical Industries reported a consolidated revenue from operations of ₹14,611.79 Crore. This represents a year-on-year growth of 12.8% compared to the ₹12,958.88 Crore reported in Q4 FY25, driven by a strong performance in the domestic branded formulations and global innovative medicines portfolios.
*   **Domestic Formulations Sales:** Rose 14.8% year-on-year to ₹4,835.90 Crore, accounting for 33.2% of consolidated sales.
*   **Innovative Medicines Sales:** Stood at $354 million, up 20.1% year-on-year and accounting for 22.2% of consolidated sales.

## PAT
The company reported a consolidated Net Profit After Tax (PAT) of ₹2,714.03 Crore for Q4 FY26, up 26.2% compared to ₹2,149.88 Crore in the corresponding quarter of the previous fiscal year. The profit growth was supported by steady operations and exceptional tax and merger credits recorded during the final months of the fiscal year.

## EPS
For the quarter ended March 31, 2026, basic Earnings Per Share (EPS) stood at ₹11.31, up from ₹8.96 in Q4 FY25. For the full fiscal year ended March 31, 2026, consolidated basic EPS was reported at ₹47.83.

## Key operational highlights
*   **Innovative Segment Transition:** The specialty business, officially renamed as "Innovative Medicines" during the quarter, achieved double-digit growth, led by specialty products *Ilumya* and *Cequa*.
*   **EBITDA and Margins:** Consolidated EBITDA for the quarter stood at ₹3,954.20 Crore, up 6.4% year-on-year. EBITDA margins came in at 27.1%, down from 30.3% for the full year due to higher-than-expected marketing expenses and R&D activities.
*   **R&D Expenditures:** Sun Pharma invested ₹975.70 Crore in R&D during the quarter, representing 6.7% of total sales.
*   **India Market Leadership:** Sun Pharma retained its position as the No. 1 player in the Indian pharmaceutical market, growing its domestic volume by 6% compared to the Indian Pharmaceutical Market (IPM) volume growth of 1.6%.
*   **Proposed Dividend:** The board recommended a final dividend of ₹5.00 per share, taking the total dividend for the fiscal year to ₹16.00 per share.

## Management commentary
Kirti Ganorkar, Managing Director of the company, shared his views during the Q4 FY26 earnings call:

"Our full-year performance reflects several significant achievements. Sun's 0.3 percentage point gain in the India market is our highest gain since the Ranbaxy acquisition, driven by our volume growth. Our US Innovative Medicines business has surpassed USD 1 billion in revenues, while Ex-US Innovative Medicines continues to demonstrate strong growth momentum. 

During the fourth quarter, we maintained double-digit topline growth of 13.6% and a 26.2% rise in net profit, supported by the steady launch of 11 new products in India. Although our quarterly EBITDA margins were slightly lower due to higher operating expenses and targeted marketing spends, our underlying cash flow generation remains strong. The recently announced Organon acquisition is expected to further accelerate Sun's transformation into a leading global pharmaceutical company, expanding our capabilities in women’s health and biosimilars."

## Future guidance
*   **Innovative Medicines Focus:** Following the Organon buyout, the company plans to increase the revenue contribution of innovative medicines to approximately 26% of overall sales, driving structural margin expansions.
*   **AUM and Funding Structure:** For the $11.75 billion Organon acquisition, management is setting up a funding mix of available cash reserves and offshore bank loans ranging from $3 billion to $4 billion, targeting deal closure by early 2027.
*   **Margin Trajectory:** In the medium term, the company expects operating margins to hover between 29% and 31% as near-term marketing investments for new launches normalize.
*   **CapEx and R&D:** Management plans to keep annual R&D spend in the range of 6% to 7% of sales, focusing on clinical trials for its biosimilars and dermatology pipeline.
