## Company overview
Sun Pharmaceutical Industries Limited is India's largest pharmaceutical company and the fourth-largest specialty generic pharmaceutical company globally. Founded in 1983 by Dilip Shanghvi, the company is headquartered in Mumbai, Maharashtra. Sun Pharma provides high-quality, affordable medicines trusted by healthcare professionals and patients in over 100 countries. The company operates more than 40 manufacturing facilities worldwide, producing generics, branded generics, specialty products, over-the-counter (OTC) remedies, and active pharmaceutical ingredients (APIs). Guided by Managing Director Kirti Ganorkar, Sun Pharma is undergoing a major strategic pivot from basic generics to high-margin "Innovative Medicines" (formerly known as the specialty business). This evolution was highlighted in April 2026 by the signing of a definitive agreement to acquire US-based Organon & Co. for an enterprise value of $11.75 billion, marking the largest outbound acquisition in Indian pharmaceutical history.

## Business segments
*   **India Branded Formulations:** The bankable core of Sun Pharma's domestic operations, where it holds a market-leading share of approximately 8.4%. This segment comprises chronic, sub-chronic, and acute therapeutic therapies.
*   **US Formulations (Generics & Innovative Medicines):** Comprises a diversified generics portfolio and a fast-growing, high-margin Innovative Medicines business, which crossed the landmark $1 billion revenue milestone in FY26.
*   **Emerging Markets & Rest of the World (RoW):** Spans across over 80 markets in Latin America, Asia, Africa, and Eastern Europe, delivering branded generic formulations tailored to regional healthcare infrastructures.
*   **Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients (APIs):** Produces bulk drug substances for captive consumption in formulation manufacturing as well as third-party sales to global pharmaceutical innovators and generic companies.
*   **Innovative Medicines / Specialty Portfolio:** Focuses on proprietary, patent-protected, or clinically differentiated treatments across dermatology, ophthalmology, oncology, and urology.

## Products
*   **Innovative / Specialty Medications:** Includes high-growth therapeutics such as *Ilumya* (plaque psoriasis), *Cequa* (dry eye disease), *Winlevi* (acne), *Odomzo* (basal cell carcinoma), and the recently launched *Unloxcyt* (skin cancer therapy).
*   **Branded Generics (India & Emerging Markets):** Leading brand therapies across cardiology, psychiatry, neurology, gastroenterology, diabetology, and respiratory medicine.
*   **New Product Formulations:** Includes the newly introduced Semaglutide formulations (*Noveltreat* and *Sematrinity*) launched in March 2026 to address metabolic disorders and obesity management.
*   **Active Ingredients:** Underpins formulation stability by producing complex bulk molecules including statins, antihistamines, and anti-inflammatories.

## Financial highlights
Consolidated financial metrics for the full year ended March 31, 2026, show steady operating metrics:
*   **Consolidated Sales:** ₹58,220.10 Crore (₹582,201 million), registering a year-on-year growth of 11.9%.
*   **Global Innovative Medicines Sales:** Surpassed a significant milestone at $1.42 billion, up 16.4% year-on-year, accounting for 20.7% of total sales.
*   **Consolidated EBITDA:** Reached ₹17,731.40 Crore (₹177,314 million), with an EBITDA margin of 30.3%.
*   **Consolidated Net Profit (PAT):** Stood at ₹11,479.40 Crore (₹114,794 million), marking a 5.0% increase year-on-year, despite being tempered by US integration and restructuring charges.
*   **R&D Investment:** ₹3,554.00 Crore (₹35,540 million), representing approximately 6.1% of consolidated sales.
*   **Dividend Distribution:** Recommended a final dividend of ₹5.00 per share, taking the total dividend for FY26 to ₹16.00 per share (including an interim payout of ₹11.00).

## Competitive advantages
*   **Dominant Domestic Market Position:** Sun Pharma maintains its position as the No. 1 player in the high-margin Indian pharmaceutical market, gaining 0.3 percentage points in market share during FY26—its highest market share gain since the Ranbaxy acquisition.
*   **Established Global Innovative Platform:** A robust, direct commercial pipeline for Innovative Medicines in the US and Europe provides a cushion against the pricing pressures and commoditization of generic drugs.
*   **Transnational Manufacturing Footprint:** Over 40 manufacturing sites globally, with extensive USFDA, EMA, and WHO-GMP certifications, ensuring consistent supply chain flexibility and product safety compliance.
*   **Strong Balance Sheet and Deal Executability:** The company’s low debt-to-equity ratio and substantial cash reserves enabled it to announce the $11.75 billion cash acquisition of Organon in early 2026, instantly establishing a global presence in Women’s Health and Biosimilars.

## Risk factors
*   **Organon Integration and Debt Leverage:** The $11.75 billion buyout of Organon will require Sun Pharma to take on significant offshore loans (estimated between $3 billion to $4 billion), raising its leverage profile and introducing complex operational integration risks across 150 countries.
*   **US Generics Regulatory Hurdles:** Undergoing inspections by the USFDA across several domestic API and formulation facilities (e.g., Halol, Mohali). Import alerts or warning letters can delay new product launches in the US market.
*   **Pricing Pressures and Channel Consolidation:** Persistent buyer consolidation in the US generic distribution channel limits pricing power, causing high price erosion on older generic portfolios.
*   **Patent Litigation and Loss of Exclusivity:** Innovative products face aggressive generic entry challenges; any adverse legal rulings regarding patent durations for core innovative drugs could impact long-term margins.

## Management outlook
Managing Director Kirti Ganorkar and the executive board are focused on guiding Sun Pharma through its transition into an innovative global biopharma player. In the near term, the priority is completing the Organon transaction by early 2027 and setting up a diversified funding structure. The company aims to sustain a 30% to 32% EBITDA margin by expanding its specialty dermatology and ophthalmology portfolios. Additionally, management is prioritizing domestic sales, targeting high-single to double-digit growth in the Indian market, and pursuing targeted acquisitions—such as the June 2026 agreement to buy Innovcare Lifesciences—to strengthen its local portfolio.
