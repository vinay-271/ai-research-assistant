## Revenue
For the fourth quarter (Q4) of the fiscal year ended March 31, 2026, Larsen & Toubro reported consolidated revenue from operations of ₹82,762.18 Crore. This represents an 11.25% growth year-on-year compared to ₹74,392 Crore reported in Q4 FY25.
*   **Total Consolidated Income:** Stood at ₹84,340.76 Crore for the quarter.
*   **Execution Momentum:** Revenue growth was driven by steady execution across the Hi-Tech Manufacturing segment (+45% YoY) and Energy Projects (+36% YoY), which offset flatter trends in Infrastructure Projects.

## PAT
Bharti Airtel’s promoter-backed peer, L&T, reported a consolidated Profit After Tax (PAT) of ₹5,326 Crore for Q4 FY26. This represents a slight decline of 3.12% compared to ₹5,497 Crore reported in the fourth quarter of the previous fiscal year.
*   **Exceptional Base Effect:** The marginal dip in quarterly profitability was primarily due to a high base effect from an exceptional gain of ₹475 Crore in the corresponding quarter of FY25, alongside cost inflation in select domestic EPC jobs.

## EPS
For the quarter ended March 31, 2026, consolidated basic and diluted Earnings Per Share (EPS) was reported at ₹38.74 per share (normalized). For the full financial year ended March 31, 2026, the consolidated annual EPS reached ₹116.89 per share.

## Key operational highlights
*   **Quarterly Order Inflows:** Group order inflows for Q4 FY26 stood at ₹89,772 Crore, taking the total order book to an all-time high of ₹7,40,327 Crore.
*   **Operating Margins:** The consolidated operating EBITDA margin stood at 8.53% for the quarter. Margins were impacted by legacy cost inflation in older hydrocarbon projects in the Energy segment, though offset by improved profitability in tech subsidiaries and Hi-Tech manufacturing.
*   **Leadership Updates:** The Board appointed Mr. P. Ramakrishnan as Chief Financial Officer (CFO) effective July 1, 2026. He succeeds Mr. R. Shankar Raman, who will continue to serve as President and Whole-time Director – Finance.
*   **Dividend Recommendation:** The Board recommended a final dividend of ₹38 per share (face value of ₹2) for the financial year ended March 31, 2026.

## Management commentary
S. N. Subrahmanyan, Chairman and Managing Director, commented on the Q4 results:

"We concluded the financial year with a resilient performance, characterized by record order inflows of ₹4.36 trillion and a robust top-line growth of 12%. While our Q4 consolidated net profit saw a minor dip of 3% due to high base effects and commodity cost pressures, our operational parameters remain strong.

Regarding West Asia, we would like to reassure our stakeholders that our project sites are fully operational. We have not witnessed any project cancellations, and execution continues to progress steadily. As we transition into our new Lakshya '31 strategic cycle, we are positioned to capture opportunities in clean energy, semiconductor technologies, and AI-driven systems, while keeping our core EPC delivery efficient."

## Future guidance
*   **Order Inflow & Revenue Growth:** The company has guided for a consolidated order inflow and revenue growth of 10% to 12% for the upcoming fiscal year, supported by a domestic infrastructure pipeline and capital expenditure plans in West Asia.
*   **EBITDA Margin Profile:** Management expects consolidated EBITDA margins to remain in the range of 8.6% to 8.9% for the next fiscal year, with potential upside as high-cost legacy projects are completed and tech manufacturing scales up.
*   **Strategic Capex & Non-Core Exits:** Under Lakshya '31, capital allocation will prioritize digital technology, green hydrogen systems, and semiconductor packaging, while systematically reducing exposure to capital-intensive concession projects.
