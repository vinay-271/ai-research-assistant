## Company overview
ICICI Bank Limited is one of India's largest private sector banks by assets and market capitalization. Established in 1994, the bank has built a strong retail and corporate franchise with an extensive network of domestic branches, ATMs, and international representative offices. Headquartered in Mumbai, Maharashtra, ICICI Bank has transitioned over the decades from a development finance institution into a technology-driven, universal commercial bank. The bank is led by Managing Director and CEO Sandeep Bakhshi, who has overseen a focus on risk-calibrated core operating profit growth, technological leadership, and robust underwriting standards.

## Business segments
*   **Retail Banking:** The primary driver of the bank's domestic operations, offering consumer liability products, residential mortgages, personal loans, vehicle loans, credit cards, and digital-first payment solutions.
*   **Wholesale & Corporate Banking:** Caters to large conglomerates, middle-market enterprises, and institutional clients, providing project finance, working capital facilities, transaction banking, corporate trade credit, and foreign exchange hedging solutions.
*   **SME & Business Banking:** Focuses on small and medium-scale enterprises, providing customized business loans, supply chain finance, and digital merchant accounts.
*   **Treasury Operations:** Manages the bank’s investment portfolios, asset-liability matching, sovereign debt reserves (SLR), and commercial trading books.
*   **Subsidiaries & Associates:** Conducts business across general insurance (ICICI Lombard), life insurance (ICICI Prudential Life), asset management (ICICI Prudential AMC), investment banking and retail broking (following the delisting and integration of ICICI Securities).

## Products
*   **Deposit Accounts:** Savings, current, salary, and fixed deposit accounts tailored for retail, NRI, and corporate clients, as well as the specialized Capital Gains Account Scheme.
*   **Consumer & Commercial Credit:** Housing loans, personal lines of credit, gold loans, commercial vehicle financing, business expansion credit, and structured corporate loans.
*   **Cards and Digital Payments:** A suite of co-branded and premium credit/debit cards, integrated with the iMobile Pay super-app.
*   **Digital Wealth and Ecosystem Platforms:** Custom portals such as InstaBIZ for corporate merchants, digital-first NRI transaction banking platforms, and automated trade solutions.

## Financial highlights
Consolidated and standalone financial parameters for the full financial year ended March 31, 2026, demonstrate consistent growth:
*   **Annual Standalone Net Profit (PAT):** Grew 6.2% year-on-year to ₹50,147 Crore in FY26, compared to ₹47,227 Crore in FY25.
*   **Annual Standalone NII:** Rose to ₹88,075 Crore, driven by credit expansion.
*   **Core Operating Profit:** Increased by 7.7% year-on-year to ₹70,401 Crore.
*   **Balance Sheet Assets:** Standalone advances grew 15.8% year-on-year to ₹15,53,893 Crore, while standalone period-end deposits grew 11.4% to ₹17,94,625 Crore.
*   **Asset Quality:** Standalone Gross NPA (GNPA) fell to 1.40%, and Net NPA (NNPA) ratio improved to 0.33% as of March 31, 2026.
*   **Capital Strength:** standalone Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR) stood at 17.18%, with a Common Equity Tier 1 (CET-1) ratio of 16.35%.

## Competitive advantages
*   **Digital Transformation Leadership:** Driven by its "iMobile Pay" application, the bank continues to be an early adopter of payments technology, introducing fingerprint and facial recognition biometrics to secure high-volume UPI transactions.
*   **Strong Retail Deposit Franchise:** A robust CASA (Current Account Savings Account) franchise, averaging 38.6% in Q4 FY26, provides a reliable source of low-cost funds.
*   **Risk-Calibrated Underwriting:** Under Sandeep Bakhshi’s leadership, the bank has focused on granular, well-rated retail and SME loan segments, lowering systemic credit costs.
*   **Synergy Across Subsidiaries:** Strong market positions in life/general insurance and asset management provide diversified fee-income and extensive cross-selling opportunities across the parent group.

## Risk factors
*   **Liability Cost and Margin Pressures:** Rising interest rates and intense competition across the industry for retail deposits may put pressure on Net Interest Margins (NIMs) in future quarters.
*   **Systemic Delinquency in Priority Sectors:** Despite improved asset quality, agricultural and priority-sector lending books require consistent monitoring. The bank holds substantial contingency provisions against potential slippages.
*   **Operational and Cybersecurity Risks:** As transaction volumes on digital networks scale up, protecting system uptimes and preventing digital fraud remains an ongoing operational priority.
*   **Global Macroeconomic Exposures:** Foreign exchange and interest rate volatility could affect trading income, treasury valuations, and offshore lending books.

## Management outlook
The management team remains focused on delivering consistent, risk-calibrated core operating profit growth. Following the central bank’s approval of MD & CEO Sandeep Bakhshi’s reappointment until October 2028, the executive priorities center on maintaining NIMs around 4.30%, leveraging digital channels to lower operating costs, and growing corporate loans selectively. The bank plans to further integrate its delisted subsidiary, ICICI Securities, into its overall wealth management segment and prudently deploy capital while maintaining a strong capital adequacy buffer.
