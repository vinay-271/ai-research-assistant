## Revenue
For the fourth quarter (Q4) ended March 31, 2026, State Bank of India reported standalone financial metrics:
*   **Total Income:** Standalone total income rose to ₹1,23,098 Crore, compared to ₹1,19,509 Crore in the corresponding quarter of the previous fiscal year.
*   **Net Interest Income (NII):** Grew 4.1% year-on-year to ₹44,380 Crore, compared to ₹42,618 Crore in Q4 FY25, driven by steady credit advances.

## PAT
The bank reported a standalone Net Profit After Tax (PAT) of ₹19,684 Crore for Q4 FY26, up 5.58% compared to the standalone net profit of ₹18,643 Crore in Q4 FY25. On a sequential basis, standalone PAT declined from the high of ₹21,028 Crore recorded in Q3 FY26, primarily due to rising interest expenses and compressed treasury income.

## EPS
For the quarter ended March 31, 2026, standalone basic and diluted Earnings Per Share (EPS) stood at ₹22.06, compared to ₹20.89 in the corresponding quarter of the previous fiscal year.

## Key operational highlights
*   **Business Velocity:** Total advances stood at ₹49.30 Lakh Crore (up 16.87% YoY), driven by 17.11% retail growth, 20.99% SME growth, and 14.83% corporate expansion. Total deposits rose 11.03% YoY to stand at ₹59.80 Lakh Crore.
*   **CASA Ratio:** Maintained at 39.46% as of March 31, 2026, with CASA deposits growing by 9.53% year-on-year.
*   **Margin Compression:** Net interest margins faced pressure; domestic NIM for Q4 FY26 contracted to 2.93% (and whole bank NIM stood at 2.81%), down from 3.14% in Q4 FY25. This contraction contributed to subsequent stock price volatility.
*   **Pristine Asset Quality:** Standalone Gross NPA improved significantly to 1.49% (down 33 bps YoY), while Net NPA ratio was managed at 0.39%. Provision coverage ratio (PCR) stood at 74.36%.
*   **Digital Channels:** Over 98.7% of the bank's total transactions during the quarter were processed via alternate digital channels, with YONO facilitating more than 66% of new savings account openings.
*   **Capital Position:** Capital Adequacy Ratio (CRAR) as of March 31, 2026, stood at a comfortable 15.40%.

## Management commentary
Chairman CS Setty commented on the results:

"Our Q4 FY26 results demonstrate stable performance, with quarterly profits rising to ₹19,684 crore and full-year profits crossing the ₹80,000 crore mark. Credit demand remains steady across retail, SME, and corporate segments, allowing us to maintain a whole-bank advances growth of nearly 17%. 

While the high-cost deposit environment has led to a compression in our domestic NIM to 2.93%, our asset quality has improved further, with GNPA down to 1.49% and credit cost at a comfortable 0.27%. Alternate channels now handle 98.7% of our transactions, and we are prioritizing technology investments to establish a cybersecurity defense center. We remain confident in our credit growth targets and will continue focusing on retail deposit mobilization to support our balance sheet expansion."

## Future guidance
*   **Credit and Deposit Growth:** The bank maintains its credit growth guidance of 13% to 15% for FY27, backed by strong corporate capital expenditure pipelines and stable retail loan demand.
*   **NIM Management:** SBI expects Net Interest Margins (NIM) to stabilize in the 2.90% to 3.00% range as deposit pricing begins to peak.
*   **Asset Quality and Credit Costs:** Management aims to keep the Gross NPA ratio below 1.50% and maintain credit cost at under 0.50% over the next financial year.
*   **Digital Scaling:** The company plans to continue upgrading the YONO platform, focusing on enhancing digital credit products and improving digital-only savings account acquisitions.
