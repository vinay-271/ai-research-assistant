## Company overview
Infosys Limited is a global leader in next-generation digital services, AI-first business consulting, and technology services. Co-founded in Pune, India in 1981 by N. R. Narayana Murthy and six other computer professionals, the company is now headquartered in Bengaluru, Karnataka. Over more than four decades, Infosys has established itself as an IT services provider operating in over 50 countries, facilitating enterprise digital transformations for clients across finance, retail, telecom, and manufacturing. The company is led by Managing Director and CEO Salil Parekh.

## Business segments
*   **Financial Services:** The bank's largest revenue contributor, providing digital banking transformation, core software solutions, risk management, and cloud enablement for banks, insurance firms, and capital markets.
*   **Retail, CPG & Logistics:** Focuses on e-commerce platforms, hyper-personalized supply chains, automated warehouse management, and data-driven customer loyalty portfolios.
*   **Communications, Media & Technology (CMT):** Delivers next-generation solutions for telecom operators, streaming platforms, digital publishers, and technology product developers.
*   **Energy, Utilities, Resources & Services (EURS):** Provides smart grid solutions, carbon emission tracking applications, pipeline management systems, and enterprise asset management.
*   **Manufacturing:** Caters to automotive, industrial, and aerospace clients, focusing on industrial IoT (IIOT), predictive maintenance systems, and Product Lifecycle Management (PLM) integrations.
*   **Life Sciences & Healthcare:** Services pharmaceutical firms, medical device manufacturers, and health insurance providers with clinical data systems, compliance automation, and healthcare informatics.

## Products
*   **Infosys Topaz:** An AI-first offering leveraging generative and agentic AI technologies to automate complex business processes and deliver cognitive enterprise solutions.
*   **Infosys Cobalt:** A comprehensive set of cloud-first services, platforms, and software templates designed to accelerate public, private, and hybrid cloud migrations.
*   **Finacle:** An industry-leading core banking platform used by financial institutions across more than 100 countries to manage deposits, lending, digital banking, and treasury operations.
*   **EdgeVerve:** A product suite focused on artificial intelligence, robotic process automation (RPA), and structured document extraction (under platforms like AssistEdge and XtractEdge).
*   **Panaya:** A software-as-a-service (SaaS) suite delivering change impact analysis and automated testing for SAP, Oracle, and Salesforce applications.

## Financial highlights
Consolidated financial parameters for the fiscal year ended March 31, 2026, indicate solid operational performance:
*   **Consolidated Revenue:** Reached $20,158 million (approximately ₹1,78,650 Crore) for FY26, representing a growth of 3.1% in constant currency and 9.6% in rupee terms over the previous fiscal year.
*   **Consolidated Net Profit (PAT):** Stood at ₹29,474 Crore, marking a 10.2% increase compared to ₹26,713 Crore in FY25.
*   **EBIT Margin Profile:** Reported IFRS operating margin stood at 20.3%, while adjusted operating margin reached 21.0%.
*   **Large Deal TCV:** Large deal wins reached a total contract value (TCV) of $14.9 billion, with net new deals accounting for 55% of the portfolio.
*   **Free Cash Flow (FCF):** Free cash flow generation was robust at $3,733 million (approximately ₹31,000 Crore).

## Competitive advantages
*   **AI-First Delivery Framework:** The systematic integration of Infosys Topaz across all traditional offerings enables the company to transition from reactive labor-led IT support to proactive, predictive AI automation.
*   **Scale and Client Footprint:** A strong presence in the Fortune 500 network, with deep domain expertise across financial services, telecom, and industrial manufacturing, provides a stable recurring revenue base.
*   **Global Delivery Infrastructure:** Standardized delivery pipelines, high-security onshore-offshore development centers, and localized regional hubs optimize delivery costs and timeline.
*   **Reskilling Program:** Rapid and structured internal training programs designed to transition thousands of employees onto open-source, proprietary, and pre-built generative AI foundation models.

## Risk factors
*   **Discretionary Spend Softness:** Continued high interest rates and macro headwinds in key North American and European geographies have restricted clients' non-essential discretionary IT spend, leading to longer sales cycles.
*   **Regulatory Gratuity and Benefit Exposures:** Changes in domestic labor frameworks, such as India's notified labor codes, can create significant one-off liabilities related to gratuity and past service cost amendments.
*   **Specialized Skill Compensation:** The ongoing race for generative AI and machine learning engineering talent may inflate employee cost structures and affect operating margins.
*   **Exchange Rate Volatility:** Extreme fluctuations in global currency pairs (specifically USD, EUR, and GBP against the INR) directly affect reported revenues and operating margins.

## Management outlook
Infosys' management maintains a disciplined approach to capital execution and operational efficiency. For the upcoming fiscal year (FY27), the company has guided for constant currency revenue growth of 1.5% to 3.5%, reflecting a conservative yet stable outlook on global client spend. The target operating margin for FY27 is projected in the range of 20% to 22%, supported by the optimization of sub-contractor costs and AI-driven automation. To sustain long-term execution, the company has targeted the induction of approximately 20,000 freshers over the course of the next financial year.
