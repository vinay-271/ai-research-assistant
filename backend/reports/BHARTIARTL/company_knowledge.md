## Company overview
Bharti Airtel Limited, established in 1995 by Sunil Bharti Mittal, is a leading global telecommunications company headquartered in New Delhi, India. It ranks among the top three mobile service providers globally by subscriber volume, with operations spanning 15 countries across South Asia and Africa. The company manages a massive subscriber base of over 666 million users globally, including more than 482 million in India. Airtel is recognized for its premium subscriber mix, extensive high-speed 5G network footprint, and its transition into a digital services conglomerate offering broadband, enterprise solutions, digital payments, and cloud infrastructure.

## Business segments
*   **Mobile Services (India):** The main revenue contributor, delivering 2G, 4G, and 5G wireless voice and data services to retail consumers across all 22 telecom circles in India.
*   **Homes Services:** Focuses on high-speed broadband (FTTH), Fixed Wireless Access (FWA under "Xstream AirFiber"), and internet-based television services (IPTV) for residential users.
*   **Airtel Business (Enterprise Services):** Provides comprehensive ICT solutions to corporates, government institutions, and small businesses, encompassing connectivity, cloud, cybersecurity, data centers (Nxtra), IoT, and CPaaS (Airtel IQ).
*   **Digital TV Services:** Operates direct-to-home (DTH) satellite television services across India under the "Airtel Digital TV" brand.
*   **Mobile Services (Africa):** Managed via the publicly listed subsidiary Airtel Africa PLC, providing wireless voice, data, and mobile commerce (Airtel Money) services in 14 African countries.

## Products
*   **Wireless Mobile Connections:** Prepaid and postpaid mobile plans featuring unlimited voice, high-speed data, and bundled content streaming packages.
*   **Airtel Xstream Fiber & AirFiber:** High-speed fiber-optic broadband and 5G-based fixed wireless broadband solutions.
*   **Digital TV (DTH):** High-definition and Android-enabled smart set-top boxes with access to domestic and international television channels.
*   **Airtel Payments Bank:** A digital-first banking platform offering savings accounts, remittance facilities, utility payments, and micro-lending integration.
*   **Enterprise ICT Portfolio:** Sovereign cloud solutions, unified communication engines, SD-WAN, multi-tenant IoT connectivity, and secure digital transaction infrastructure.

## Financial highlights
Consolidated financial metrics for the full year ended March 31, 2026, highlight steady growth across domestic and international operations:
*   **Annual Consolidated Revenue:** ₹2,10,973 Crore, registering a year-on-year growth of 22.0% compared to FY25.
*   **Consolidated EBITDA:** Reached ₹1,21,268 Crore, with an EBITDA margin of 57.5% (up 3.0% YoY).
*   **India Operations EBITDA:** Rose to ₹93,197 Crore, showing an EBITDA margin of 60.1% (up 3.5% YoY).
*   **Consolidated Net Income (Before Exceptional Items):** Stood at ₹26,904 Crore, indicating strong underlying operational profitability.
*   **Credit Rating Upgrade:** On June 24, 2026, S&P Global Ratings upgraded Bharti Airtel’s long-term issuer credit rating to "BBB+" from "BBB," reflecting disciplined balance sheet management, strong cash flow, and reduced debt leverage.

## Competitive advantages
*   **Industry-Leading ARPU:** Maintains India's highest Average Revenue Per User (ARPU) at ₹257, driven by a deliberate strategy to attract premium subscribers and upgrade 2G users to smartphone data plans.
*   **Premium Postpaid Franchise:** Commands a leading position in the high-yield postpaid segment, with a customer base of approximately 29 million postpaid connections.
*   **Geographic Diversification:** A profitable and growing African operation (Airtel Africa) provides a hedge against domestic market fluctuations, with constant-currency revenue in Africa growing at over 22% annually.
*   **Comprehensive Digital Ecosystem:** Strong cross-selling opportunities across broadband, payments, enterprise services, and smart television setups help improve customer retention and reduce churn.

## Risk factors
*   **Intense Domestic Competition:** Rivalry with other private operators for market share requires ongoing promotions, which may limit immediate tariff hikes.
*   **Significant Capital Commitments:** The expansion of 5G infrastructure, fiber deployment, and upcoming spectrum renewals require substantial capital expenditure, which could impact free cash flow generation.
*   **Foreign Exchange Volatility in Africa:** Currency devaluations in key African markets, such as the Nigerian Naira, can affect reported consolidated rupee earnings despite strong constant-currency performance.
*   **Regulatory Policy Shifts:** Changes in regulatory fees, spectrum pricing, or compliance norms implemented by the Department of Telecommunications (DoT) can impact cash flows.

## Management outlook
Bharti Airtel’s leadership is led by Executive Vice Chairman Gopal Vittal and MD & CEO Shashwat Sharma, who assumed the role on January 1, 2026. Management's medium-term strategy focuses on driving ARPU toward the ₹300 level through portfolio premiumization and tariff adjustments. The company is prioritizing capital allocation toward high-growth segments, including the expansion of its "Xstream AirFiber" fixed wireless broadband and the scaling of its corporate data center business (Nxtra). Bharti Airtel also aims to reduce its environmental impact, collaborating with partner Indus Towers to transition cellular towers away from diesel usage toward high-power battery storage and solar energy.
