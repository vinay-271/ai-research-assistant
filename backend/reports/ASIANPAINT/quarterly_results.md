## Revenue
For the fourth quarter (Q4) of the fiscal year ended March 31, 2026, Asian Paints reported a consolidated revenue from operations of ₹9,246.70 Crore. This represents a 10.6% year-on-year growth compared to the ₹8,358.90 Crore reported in Q4 of the previous fiscal year. The revenue growth was primarily driven by volume expansion in the domestic decorative segment, along with stable performance in the industrial paint division, which offset relatively muted real estate trends in major urban centers.

## PAT
Consolidated Profit After Tax (PAT) for Q4 FY26 was ₹1,172.12 Crore, reflecting a 69.3% increase compared to ₹692.13 Crore in the corresponding quarter of the previous fiscal year. This sharp profit recovery was supported by a lower comparative base in Q4 FY25, favorable raw material prices during early parts of the quarter, and ongoing cost optimization programs across manufacturing units.

## EPS
For the quarter ended March 31, 2026, the company's Basic and Diluted Earnings Per Share (EPS) stood at ₹12.22, up from ₹7.22 in Q4 FY25. For the full year FY26, consolidated basic EPS rose to ₹45.10.

## Key operational highlights
*   **Decorative Volume Growth:** The domestic decorative business registered a 12.4% volume growth year-on-year, indicating a recovery in consumer demand across both economy and luxury emulsions.
*   **Industrial Segment Growth:** The industrial coatings business, operated through joint ventures with PPG, recorded value growth of 11.0% and volume growth of 12.7%, driven by steady demand from the automotive OEM and protective coatings sectors.
*   **Gross Margin Improvement:** Consolidated gross margin expanded to 44.8%, up by 87 basis points compared to the same period last year, primarily due to moderate cost trends for petrochemical key components and raw material formulation adjustments.
*   **Home Décor Footprint:** The company expanded its specialized retail format, the "Beautiful Homes Store", further establishing its presence across multiple tier-2 and tier-3 cities to support multi-category product lines.

## Management commentary
"Our performance this quarter reflects steady demand across our main decorative and industrial lines. The domestic decorative business achieved healthy double-digit volume growth of 12.4%, showing that our focus on product availability, brand investments, and a wider distribution network continues to support volume growth. 

While the home decor segment saw relatively quiet demand, we continued to expand our 'Beautiful Homes' ecosystem. Our operating margins improved this quarter due to moderate raw material costs and consistent cost discipline, even as we worked to manage competitive pressures. Looking ahead, we will continue focusing on volume growth and brand strength to deliver long-term value."

## Future guidance
*   **Operating Margins:** The company aims to keep consolidated operating EBITDA margins within the 18% to 20% range over the medium term, adjusting pricing to balance profitability with volume growth.
*   **Volume Metrics:** Management expects to maintain positive volume momentum, targeting high-single to low-double-digit growth in the domestic decorative business, supported by a normal monsoon and rural demand.
*   **Capital Expenditure:** The company plans a capital outlay of approximately ₹2,000 Crore for the next fiscal year, focused on phased manufacturing expansions, brownfield improvements, and backward integration.
*   **Dividend Payout:** The board intends to maintain its historical dividend payout ratio of around 60%, returning surplus cash to shareholders while retaining adequate reserves for capital expansion.
