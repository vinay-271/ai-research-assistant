## Revenue
For the fourth quarter (Q4) of the fiscal year ended March 31, 2026, Axis Bank reported a total income of ₹38,746 Crore, representing an increase of 1.9% compared to the ₹38,015 Crore reported in Q4 of the previous fiscal year. 
*   **Net Interest Income (NII):** NII grew 5% year-on-year (and 1% sequentially) to ₹14,457 Crore.
*   **Fee Income:** Fee income stood at ₹6,561 Crore, registering an increase of 4% year-on-year and 8% sequentially. Retail fees constituted approximately 92% of the overall fee income for the quarter.

## PAT
The bank reported a standalone Net Profit After Tax (PAT) of ₹7,071.31 Crore for Q4 FY26, down slightly by 0.6% from ₹7,117.50 Crore in Q4 FY25, but up 9% sequentially from Q3 FY26's PAT of ₹6,489.57 Crore. The profitability was impacted by higher provisioning, which rose 56.8% year-on-year to ₹3,522 Crore. This included a voluntary, one-time standard asset provision of ₹2,001 Crore to strengthen the balance sheet against global macroeconomic uncertainties.

## EPS
For the quarter ended March 31, 2026, the standalone Basic and Diluted Earnings Per Share (EPS) stood at ₹22.93, up from the sequential quarter's EPS of ₹21.04. For the full fiscal year ended March 31, 2026, basic EPS reached ₹79.40.

## Key operational highlights
*   **Deposit Growth:** Total deposits grew 14% year-on-year and 6% quarter-on-quarter. CASA deposits grew by a healthy 7% sequentially, helping ease overall funding pressures.
*   **Credit Growth:** Loan book expansion remained strong at ~19% year-on-year. SME loans grew 24% YoY, and Corporate loans rose 38% YoY, led by a 33% YoY increase in mid-corporate credit.
*   **Asset Quality Improvement:** Gross NPA ratio improved to 1.23% from 1.40% in Q3 FY26, while Net NPA declined by 5 basis points sequentially to 0.37%. Credit cost was stable, with net credit cost annualized at 0.37% (down 39 bps sequentially).
*   **Credit Cards and Payments:** Axis Bank issued 1.24 million new credit cards during Q4 FY26, maintaining its market share of approximately 14% in cards in force.
*   **Capital Planning:** The Board of Directors approved a potential capital raise of up to ₹55,000 Crore through equity and debt instruments, subject to regulatory and shareholder approvals.

## Management commentary
Amitabh Chaudhry, Managing Director and CEO, commented on the quarter's performance:

"During Q4 of FY26, we took the initiative to further strengthen our balance sheet by voluntarily enhancing our prudent provisioning framework for standard assets, creating a one-time additional provision of ₹2,001 Crore. This measure is entirely precautionary and reflects our conservative risk management philosophy in the face of volatile global conditions. 

Our core operational performance across deposits, SME, and corporate advances has been highly encouraging, with total deposits growing by 6% and advances showing steady traction. Our asset quality remains strong, with GNPA improving to 1.23% and credit costs remaining well in check. We remain committed to our priorities of digital excellence, retail and wholesale deposit gathering, and customer obsession under our Sparsh initiative."

## Future guidance
*   **Balance Sheet and Margins:** Management expects Net Interest Margin (NIM) to stabilize within a range of 3.60% to 3.70% in the upcoming quarters as deposit costs begin to peak.
*   **Loan and Deposit Growth:** The bank intends to align loan growth closer to deposit mobilization to keep the loan-to-deposit ratio sustainable, targeting credit growth of 17% to 19% in FY27.
*   **Unsecured Lending Strategy:** The bank will maintain a selective and disciplined underwriting approach for unsecured retail books, especially credit cards and micro-business loans, while continuing to expand corporate and SME portfolios.
*   **Credit Costs:** Net credit costs are expected to remain below 0.50% over the next financial year, supported by clean corporate books and stabilizing retail delinquency trends.
