## Company overview
Axis Bank Limited, formerly known as UTI Bank, was established in 1993 as one of the first new-generation private sector banks in India. Headquartered in Mumbai, Maharashtra, the bank rebranded to Axis Bank in 2007 to reflect its expanding footprint and evolving identity. Today, it stands as India's third-largest private sector bank, offering a comprehensive suite of financial services to customer segments spanning retail, small and medium enterprises (SMEs), and large corporate entities. Led by Managing Director and CEO Amitabh Chaudhry, Axis Bank operates thousands of domestic branches, an extensive ATM network, and several international offices, with its primary core strategy focused on "Bharat Banking" (rural and semi-urban integration), digital transformation, and a customer-centric program known as "Sparsh."

## Business segments
*   **Retail Banking:** This segment comprises personal banking services, including liability accounts (savings, current, and term deposits), retail lending (mortgages, auto loans, personal loans), cards, wealth management, and distribution of third-party insurance and mutual fund products.
*   **Corporate & Wholesale Banking:** Caters to large conglomerates, public sector undertakings, and mid-sized corporate clients. It provides working capital finance, term loans, project finance, trade services, cash management solutions, capital market products, and syndication services.
*   **SME & Commercial Banking:** Focuses on small and medium enterprises and medium-scale commercial enterprises. This segment addresses businesses' unique needs through structured trade products, supply chain finance, working capital, and term facilities.
*   **Treasury Operations:** Manages the bank’s liquidity, sovereign and corporate investment portfolios, and foreign exchange trading, while offering hedging and derivative products to corporate clients.

## Products
*   **Deposit Accounts:** A wide range of savings products, current accounts, and high-yield fixed/recurring deposit schemes tailored for retail, premium (Burgundy), and business customers.
*   **Retail Lending:** Unsecured and secured lending products such as home loans, personal loans, loans against property, educational loans, and auto loans.
*   **Cards and Payments:** Extensive suite of credit cards (co-branded cards with brands like IndiGo, Airtel, and Flipkart), debit cards, and prepaid cards. Axis Bank is a leading UPI Payer PSP and merchant-acquiring player.
*   **Corporate Banking Products:** Customized debt syndication, trade finance, cash management solutions, treasury services, and capital market advisory services.
*   **Subsidiary Products:** Through its subsidiaries, the bank provides mutual funds (Axis AMC), stock broking and retail trading (Axis Securities), investment banking and advisory (Axis Capital), and non-banking financial services (Axis Finance).

## Financial highlights
Axis Bank's consolidated financial results for the fiscal year ended March 31, 2026, show steady core business metrics:
*   **Net Profit (PAT):** Annual consolidated PAT stood at ₹24,457 Crore, down 7% year-on-year, primarily due to higher prudent provisions, including a voluntary, one-time standard asset provision of ₹2,001 Crore in Q4.
*   **Net Interest Income (NII):** Annual NII reached ₹56,048 Crore, growing 3% year-on-year.
*   **Fee Income:** Rose 9% year-on-year to ₹24,444 Crore, driven by granular retail fees, which accounted for approximately 92% of total fees.
*   **Operating Profit:** Annual operating profit grew 2% year-on-year to ₹42,817 Crore, while core operating profit reached ₹41,443 Crore (up 4% YoY).
*   **Asset Quality:** Gross Non-Performing Asset (GNPA) ratio improved to 1.23% (down 5 bps YoY), and Net NPA (NNPA) ratio stood at 0.37%.
*   **Capital Position:** Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR) stood at 16.42%, with a Common Equity Tier 1 (CET-1) ratio of 14.38%.

## Competitive advantages
*   **Digital Leadership:** Axis Bank maintains a strong digital presence, holding a 36% market share in the UPI Payer PSP space by volume and value. Its mobile application ("open") has high customer ratings and approximately 16 million monthly active users.
*   **Credit Cards and Payments Engine:** Possesses a 14% market share in credit cards in force, driven by high-traction co-branded partnerships. Additionally, the bank is a dominant player in merchant acquiring, with a terminal market share of approximately 22.4%.
*   **"Bharat Banking" Strategy:** Axis Bank’s targeted approach to rural and semi-urban markets has established a large network of branches, business correspondents, and digital channels, allowing it to capture untapped credit and deposit demand.
*   **Integrated Subsidiary Offerings:** The bank cross-sells services through market-leading subsidiaries like Axis AMC, Axis Securities, Axis Capital, and Axis Finance, generating diversified fee-income streams.

## Risk factors
*   **Unsecured Retail Exposure:** A rapid expansion in unsecured products, including personal loans and credit cards, exposes the bank to credit stress if macroeconomic conditions or repayment behaviors in the retail segment deteriorate.
*   **Deposit Pricing and Liability Pressure:** The highly competitive environment for deposits across the Indian banking sector has increased deposit costs, putting pressure on Net Interest Margins (NIMs).
*   **Cybersecurity and IT Systems Risk:** As digital banking volumes and UPI transactions scale rapidly, the bank faces risks related to system downtimes, technical declines, and sophisticated cyberattacks.
*   **Global Macroeconomic Shifts:** Heightened geopolitical tensions and changing interest rate cycles domestically and globally can affect treasury income, foreign exchange trading profits, and corporate credit demand.

## Management outlook
Axis Bank's management maintains a cautious yet progressive outlook for the medium term. Under CEO Amitabh Chaudhry, the immediate priorities include stabilizing Net Interest Margins (NIMs), reducing the credit cost, and maintaining a healthy balance between loan growth and deposit accumulation. The bank intends to expand its deposit franchise further, prioritizing granular CASA (Current Account Savings Account) growth. Management also plans to utilize its newly approved capital raising capacity of up to ₹55,000 Crore to fund long-term growth across retail, SME, and mid-corporate credit portfolios.
