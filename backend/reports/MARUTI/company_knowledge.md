## Company overview
Maruti Suzuki India Limited (MSIL), formerly known as Maruti Udyog Limited, was established in 1981 as a joint venture between the Government of India and Suzuki Motor Corporation (SMC), Japan. Rebranding to its current name in 2007, the company is India's largest passenger vehicle manufacturer, commanding a market share of over 40% in the highly competitive domestic market. Headquartered in New Delhi, the company has achieved cumulative domestic sales exceeding 30 million units. SMC currently holds a 56.28% equity stake in Maruti Suzuki, making it Suzuki's largest subsidiary globally by both production volume and sales. The company operates state-of-the-art manufacturing facilities in Gurugram, Manesar, and a newly developing mega-complex in Kharkhoda, Haryana, in addition to utilizing manufacturing capacities at Suzuki Motor Gujarat (SMG).

## Business segments
*   **Passenger Vehicles:** This represents the main business core, comprising:
    *   *Mini & Compact Cars:* Entry-level and family hatchbacks.
    *   *Mid-Size Sedans:* Premium executive sedans.
    *   *Utility Vehicles (UVs):* SUVs, crossovers, and multi-purpose vehicles (MPVs), which represent a fast-growing portion of the portfolio.
    *   *Vans:* Versatile cargo and passenger vans.
*   **Light Commercial Vehicles (LCVs):** Small cargo trucks designed for urban distribution networks.
*   **Exports:** Manufactures and exports passenger vehicles to international markets, including Latin America, Africa, the Middle East, Europe, and neighboring South Asian countries.

## Products
*   **Mini & Compact Cars:** Alto K10, S-Presso, Celerio, WagonR, Swift, Ignis, Dzire, and Baleno.
*   **Mid-Size Sedans:** Ciaz.
*   **Utility Vehicles:** Brezza, Ertiga, XL6, Fronx, Jimny, Grand Vitara, and Invicto.
*   **Vans:** Eeco.
*   **Light Commercial Vehicles:** Super Carry.
*   **Electric Vehicles (EVs):** The newly unveiled e Vitara, Maruti's first battery electric vehicle (BEV), scheduled for formal market launch in late 2026.

## Financial highlights
Consolidated and standalone financial parameters for the full year ended March 31, 2026, show stable operating cash flows alongside moderate margin pressure:
*   **Consolidated Revenue from Operations:** ₹1,74,382 Crore (INR 1,743,820 million), registering a year-on-year growth of 20.2% compared to ₹1,45,109.9 Crore in FY25.
*   **Standalone Net Sales:** ₹1,74,369.5 Crore, up 20.2% over the previous fiscal year.
*   **Consolidated Net Profit (PAT):** Reached ₹14,679.5 Crore, compared to ₹14,500.2 Crore in FY25.
*   **Standalone Net Profit:** Reached an all-time high of ₹14,445.4 Crore.
*   **Operating EBITDA:** Stood at ₹21,450.2 Crore, representing an operating EBITDA margin of 12.3% (down from 13.9% in the previous year, due to material cost inflation).
*   **Annual Sales Volume:** Recorded record annual sales of 2,422,713 vehicles (up 8.4% YoY), with domestic sales at 1,974,939 units and exports surging to a record high of 447,774 units.
*   **Dividend:** The Board recommended a dividend of ₹140 per share (face value ₹5), representing the highest absolute payout in the company's history.

## Competitive advantages
*   **Vast Sales and Service Network:** Operates more than 4,000 sales outlets and service touchpoints across India, providing unparalleled customer service reach and high spare parts availability.
*   **Strong Resale Value and Trust:** Maruti Suzuki vehicles maintain a reputation for high fuel efficiency, low maintenance costs, and exceptional resale value in the used-car market.
*   **Strategic Pivot to SUVs:** A successful portfolio expansion into the SUV and UV segment (via Fronx, Brezza, and Grand Vitara) has allowed the company to capture profitable market share in premium categories.
*   **Fortress Balance Sheet:** Maintains a debt-free capital structure, supported by total equity of ₹1,07,156.3 Crore and substantial liquid investments, which provides a comfortable valuation floor.
*   **Strong Parentage Support:** Direct technological cooperation with Suzuki Motor Corporation ensures smooth product development and manufacturing localization.

## Risk factors
*   **Material Cost Inflation:** Vulnerable to price volatility in key manufacturing inputs (such as steel, copper, and precious metals), which can outpacing immediate pricing adjustments.
*   **Inventory Build-Up at Dealerships:** Elevated dealer stock levels toward the end of the fiscal year present risks of increased retail discounting and working capital blockages.
*   **Intense Competitive Pressures:** The Indian passenger car market faces competition from domestic and global manufacturers in the SUV and crossover segments, making customer retention challenging.
*   **EV Transition and Technology Adoption:** Navigating a multi-powertrain transition (comprising CNG, hybrid, and electric technologies) requires significant capital commitment, with uncertain near-term customer adoption rates.

## Management outlook
Maruti Suzuki's leadership, led by MD & CEO Hisashi Takeuchi and Chairman R.C. Bhargava, is targeting a domestic volume growth of approximately 10% for the upcoming fiscal year. To support this volume, the company plans to add 250,000 units of manufacturing capacity in FY27, with further expansions slated for Kharkhoda. Management intends to prioritize cost-reduction measures and product-mix optimizations to protect gross margins against raw material price volatility. The company also plans to finalize localization strategies for its battery assembly facilities ahead of the formal introduction of the e Vitara EV platform.
