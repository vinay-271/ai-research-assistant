## Revenue
For the fourth quarter (Q4) of the fiscal year ended March 31, 2026, Maruti Suzuki reported a standalone revenue from operations of ₹52,449 Crore (INR 524,490 million). This represents a 28.2% year-on-year growth compared to the ₹40,920.1 Crore reported in Q4 FY25, driven by record sales volumes, higher realisations, and a favorable product mix. Consolidated quarterly revenue rose 28.2% to ₹52,462.5 Crore.

## PAT
The standalone Net Profit After Tax (PAT) for Q4 FY26 was ₹3,590.5 Crore (INR 35,905 million), representing a 6.9% decline compared to ₹3,853 Crore in the fourth quarter of the previous fiscal year. Consolidated net profit for the quarter fell 6.45% to ₹3,659 Crore, down from ₹3,911.1 Crore in Q4 FY25. The decline in profitability was primarily attributed to mark-to-market (MTM) losses on treasury investments due to bond yield fluctuations, coupled with lower non-operating income during the quarter.

## EPS
For the quarter ended March 31, 2026, standalone basic and diluted Earnings Per Share (EPS) stood at ₹114.10, compared to ₹122.50 in the corresponding period of the previous fiscal year, reflecting the lower net profit. Standalone annual EPS for the full year reached ₹459.20.

## Key operational highlights
*   **Highest-Ever Quarterly Volumes:** Achieved its highest-ever quarterly sales at 676,209 units, representing an 11.8% year-on-year growth.
*   **Strong Domestic Sales:** Domestic sales stood at 538,994 units for the quarter.
*   **Record Export Shipments:** Export volumes reached an all-time high of 137,215 units, indicating strong international demand.
*   **Strong Operating EBIT:** Operating Profit (EBIT) grew by 30.4% year-on-year to an all-time high of ₹4,409.2 Crore (INR 44,092 million), indicating robust core operating performance despite non-operating investment losses.
*   **Substantial Pending Orders:** At the close of the financial year, Maruti Suzuki had approximately 190,000 pending customer orders, including nearly 130,000 orders in the small car segment, supported by post-GST reform demand.
*   **Healthy Dividend Announcement:** The Board recommended a final dividend of ₹140 per equity share for the fiscal year ended March 31, 2026.

## Management commentary
Hisashi Takeuchi, Managing Director and CEO, commented on the quarter's performance:

"Our core operational performance during the fourth quarter of FY26 was strong, as shown by our highest-ever quarterly sales of over 6.76 lakh units and record operating EBIT of ₹4,409 crore. The second half of the fiscal year saw a recovery in entry-level and compact car demand, supported by post-GST price corrections.

While our revenue surged by 28%, our net profit declined by 6.9% due to mark-to-market impacts on our treasury investments. However, our core earnings remain high-quality and cash-backed, with operating cash flows reaching ₹19,099 Crore for the year. Looking ahead, we will continue focusing on capacity expansion, margin stabilization through cost management, and preparing our production lines for our first electric vehicle, the e Vitara."

## Future guidance
*   **Domestic Volume Growth:** The company targets domestic volume growth of approximately 10% for the next fiscal year, which is above general industry projections.
*   **Capacity Expansion:** Maruti Suzuki plans to add 250,000 units of manufacturing capacity in FY27 to address its pending order backlog.
*   **Margin Trajectory:** Management is focusing on cost-optimization measures to offset rising raw material costs and stabilize its operating EBITDA margin back toward historical averages.
*   **EV Rollout:** The company is on track with its EV timeline, with battery assembly facilities and product validation for the e Vitara scheduled for completion in late 2026.
