## Company overview
Hindustan Unilever Limited (HUL) is India's largest fast-moving consumer goods (FMCG) company, with a heritage of over 90 years in the Indian market. Headquartered in Mumbai, Maharashtra, the company is a subsidiary of the British multinational Unilever PLC, which holds a majority stake in the business. HUL's extensive portfolio of products spans home care, personal care, beauty, and foods, touching the lives of nine out of ten Indian households. Under the leadership of Managing Director & CEO Rohit Jawa, HUL recently completed a significant restructuring by spinning off its ice cream business (Kwality Wall's) into a separately listed public entity, Kwality Wall's (India) Limited (KWIL), to focus resources on high-margin core portfolios.

## Business segments
*   **Home Care:** The largest business segment, delivering fabric wash solutions (laundry powders, liquids, and bars) and household cleaning products (dishwash liquids, surface cleaners, and disinfectants).
*   **Beauty & Wellbeing:** Focuses on premium skin care, hair care, color cosmetics, and health-and-wellness actives. This segment was strengthened in early 2025 through the strategic acquisition of *Minimalist*, a premium actives-led Indian beauty brand.
*   **Personal Care:** Comprises skin cleansing (soaps and body washes), oral care products (toothpastes and brushes), and deodorants.
*   **Foods & Refreshment (Nutrition):** Focuses on tea, coffee, health food drinks (including Horlicks and Boost), packaged foods, and culinary products. (Note: This segment excludes the ice cream business, which was demerged in December 2025).

## Products
*   **Home Care Brands:** Surf excel, Rin, Wheel, Sunlight, Vim, and Domex.
*   **Beauty & Wellbeing Brands:** Dove, Sunsilk, TRESemmé, Clinic Plus, Vaseline, Lakmé, Glow & Lovely, and Minimalist. In FY26, Sunsilk and Vaseline both crossed the ₹1,000 Crore annual turnover milestone, taking HUL's total count of ₹1,000 Crore+ brands to 20.
*   **Personal Care Brands:** Lifebuoy, Lux, Pears, Liril, Closeup, and Pepsodent.
*   **Foods & Refreshment Brands:** Brooke Bond Red Label, Taj Mahal, Taaza, Lipton, Bru, Kissan, Knorr, Horlicks, and Boost.

## Financial highlights
Consolidated financial metrics for the full financial year ended March 31, 2026, show steady operating metrics:
*   **Consolidated Turnover:** ₹63,763 Crore, growing 5.3% year-on-year compared to ₹60,573 Crore in FY25.
*   **Underlying Sales Growth (USG):** Stood at 5% for the full year, driven by a recovery in consumer volumes.
*   **Underlying Volume Growth (UVG):** Reached 4% for the full year, signaling positive demand momentum.
*   **Operating EBITDA Margin:** Maintained at 23.6%, reflecting stable input cost management.
*   **Total Shareholder Dividends:** Recommended a total dividend of ₹41 per share for the fiscal year (including interim and final payouts).

## Competitive advantages
*   **Vast Distribution & Tech Integration:** Reaches more than 9 million retail outlets across India, supported by the proprietary "Shikhar" B2B app, which enables direct digital ordering and real-time inventory management for millions of mom-and-pop (Kirana) stores.
*   **Market Leadership and Brand Equity:** Holds leading market share positions in over 80% of the categories in which it operates, supported by high consumer trust and top-of-mind brand recall.
*   **Unilever Global Backing:** Direct access to Unilever PLC’s global R&D networks, formulations, sustainable sourcing practices, and operational frameworks.
*   **Agile Portfolio Management:** Proven ability to premiumize core segments while selectively acquiring fast-growing niche brands (such as *Minimalist* in the clean beauty space).

## Risk factors
*   **Raw Material Price Fluctuations:** Vulnerable to price swings in key raw materials, particularly palm oil, crude oil derivatives (for packaging and linear alkylbenzene sulfonate), and agricultural commodities.
*   **Uneven Rural Recovery:** Rural consumption patterns are highly sensitive to monsoons and agricultural yields, presenting occasional volume pressure in lower-tier markets.
*   **Intense Local Competition:** Faced with growing competition from regional players, localized FMCG brands, and digital-first direct-to-consumer (D2C) startups.
*   **Regulatory & Compliance Shifts:** Subject to changing food safety guidelines from the FSSAI, plastic packaging recycling mandates (Extended Producer Responsibility), and local sourcing regulations.

## Management outlook
HUL's management maintains a volume-led growth strategy, prioritizing market development and brand building over near-term pricing adjustments. The company is focused on driving premiumization, accelerating its digital commerce channels, and expanding its presence in secondary and tertiary towns. Following the successful demerger of the ice cream business, management expects a 50 to 60 basis point structural improvement in HUL's operating EBITDA margins. The company aims to maintain its operating margins in the 23% to 24% band while continuing to invest in advertising and promotional activities.
