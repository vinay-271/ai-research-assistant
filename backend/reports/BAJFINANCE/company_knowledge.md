## Company overview
Bajaj Finance Limited (BFL), a subsidiary of Bajaj Finserv Limited, is one of India's largest and most diversified non-banking financial companies (NBFCs). Headquartered in Pune, Maharashtra, the company has transformed the consumer lending landscape in India through its digital-first underwriting systems, robust technology stack, and extensive nationwide distribution footprint. BFL serves a customer franchise of over 119 million users across urban and rural geographies. Guided by Vice Chairman and Managing Director Rajeev Jain, the company offers retail, SME, and commercial finance, along with public deposits and a suite of digital payment solutions.

## Business segments
*   **Consumer Lending:** The core engine of BFL, offering interest-free or low-cost EMI financing on consumer electronics, smartphones, digital products, apparel, and lifestyle accessories. It also includes personal loans, home loans (offered via its listed subsidiary, Bajaj Housing Finance), and gold loans.
*   **SME Lending:** Focuses on meeting the working capital and growth requirements of small and medium business owners, professionals (doctors, chartered accountants), and self-employed individuals through secured and unsecured loans.
*   **Commercial Lending:** Caters to mid-market corporates, auto ancillaries, pharmaceutical firms, specialty chemical companies, and financial institution groups, offering medium and long-term financing, including lease rental discounting.
*   **Rural Lending:** Tailors consumer finance and micro-business credit to rural and semi-urban customers, offering specialized loan products across agricultural and small-scale commercial setups.
*   **Deposits & Wealth Management:** Accepts public and corporate fixed deposits and distributes third-party investment products, including mutual funds and insurance.

## Products
*   **EMI Cards & Digital Wallet:** The Bajaj Finserv EMI Network Card allows consumers to buy goods on easy installments. This is integrated with the Bajaj Pay wallet and payment app.
*   **Personal and Business Loans:** Unsecured loans for salaried and self-employed individuals, including specialized professional loans.
*   **Secured Loans:** Home loans and loan against property (LAP) through Bajaj Housing Finance, alongside a growing gold loan portfolio.
*   **Newer Verticals:** Microfinance, tractor financing, and customized medical equipment financing for health professionals.
*   **Liability Products:** Retail and corporate fixed deposits offering attractive interest rates, rated stable by leading domestic credit rating agencies.

## Financial highlights
Consolidated financial metrics for the full financial year ended March 31, 2026, show steady growth across major performance parameters:
*   **Net Profit (PAT):** Annual consolidated PAT grew 14.3% year-on-year to ₹19,017 Crore, compared to ₹16,637 Crore in FY25.
*   **Net Interest Income (NII):** Increased by 21% year-on-year to ₹44,110 Crore.
*   **Assets Under Management (AUM):** Reached ₹5,09,980 Crore (₹5.09 Lakh Crore) as of March 31, 2026, marking a year-on-year growth of 22.4%.
*   **Loan Book Velocity:** Booked a record 52.45 million new loans during the fiscal year, crossing the milestone of 50 million annual loans for the first time.
*   **Dividend:** The Board recommended a final dividend of ₹6.00 per share, which includes a special payout of ₹0.60 per share linked to gains from the sale of shares in its subsidiary.

## Competitive advantages
*   **Proprietary Customer Database:** A huge customer franchise of over 119 million customers provides BFL with a highly active, pre-approved cross-sell database, significantly reducing customer acquisition costs.
*   **Underwriting and Risk Analytics:** Utilizes predictive algorithmic credit underwriting, leveraging structured and unstructured data to provide instant, automated loan decisions.
*   **Vast Merchant Footprint:** Partnered with more than 1,50,000 active retail touchpoints across consumer durables, digital products, and lifestyle stores in India.
*   **Omnichannel Ecosystem:** Seamless integration of physical point-of-sale systems with the digital Bajaj Finserv App (boasting over 82 million active net users), facilitating high-velocity customer touchpoints.

## Risk factors
*   **Delinquency Trends in SME & Unsecured Books:** Unsecured SME loans and micro-business credit lines remain sensitive to economic cycles. BFL proactively moderated its MSME loan book growth to 6% in FY26 due to rising industry-wide default trends.
*   **Yield Compression and NIM Pressure:** High funding costs and intense pricing competition from aggressive private and public sector banks have put pressure on Net Interest Margins (NIMs).
*   **Regulatory Changes:** Changes in central bank guidelines regarding risk weights on unsecured retail lending can directly impact BFL’s capital adequacy and borrowing costs.
*   **IT System Resilience:** With more than 12 million transactions processed monthly, any downtime in core digital systems can cause operational disruptions and brand impact.

## Management outlook
BFL's management plans to focus on balanced growth, targeting a sustainable AUM expansion of 21% to 23% for the next fiscal year. To navigate the high-interest-rate environment, the company is prioritizing product diversification, scaling newer segments like tractor loans, microfinance, and gold loans. Management also aims to improve operating efficiency through its "Consumer AI" strategy, while maintaining credit costs within a range of 1.45% to 1.60%. The medium-term target remains an Return on Assets (ROA) of 4.3%–4.7% and a Return on Equity (ROE) of 19%–21%.
