## Company overview
Reliance Industries Limited (RIL) is India's largest private sector conglomerate, headquartered in Mumbai, Maharashtra. Founded by Dhirubhai Ambani in 1966 as a synthetic textiles manufacturer, RIL has grown under the leadership of Chairman and Managing Director Mukesh D. Ambani into a global enterprise. RIL is the first Indian corporate entity to cross the $120 billion annual revenue mark, operating across energy, materials, retail, telecommunications, digital platforms, and green energy manufacturing. RIL’s businesses touch hundreds of millions of consumers daily, and its integrated business model provides cash flows from traditional hydrocarbon assets to fund expansions into technology, consumer retail, and clean energy ecosystems.

## Business segments
*   **Oil-to-Chemicals (O2C):** This segment comprises refining, petrochemicals, fuels, aviation turbine fuel, and chemical intermediates. The company operates the world’s largest refining complex in Jamnagar, Gujarat.
*   **Oil and Gas Exploration & Production:** Focuses on exploration and production of oil and natural gas in deepwater assets, primarily in the Krishna Godavari (KG D6) basin off the east coast of India.
*   **Retail (Reliance Retail):** The largest retail network in India, operating across grocery (Smart, Fresh), consumer electronics (Reliance Digital), fashion & lifestyle (Reliance Trends), and various digital commerce channels (Jiomart, Ajio).
*   **Digital Services (Jio Platforms):** Comprises telecommunications (Reliance Jio Infocomm), digital entertainment, high-speed fiber-optic broadband, cloud applications, and digital business services.
*   **New Energy & Materials:** A multi-billion dollar clean energy manufacturing platform developing the Dhirubhai Ambani Green Energy Giga Complex in Jamnagar to produce solar modules, storage batteries, green hydrogen, and fuel cells.

## Products
*   **Hydrocarbons and Polymers:** Gasoline, diesel, kerosene, liquefied petroleum gas (LPG), polypropylene, polyethylene, and polyester fibers.
*   **Consumer Retail Merchandise:** Groceries, apparel, personal care, home appliances, smartphones, and domestic consumer goods.
*   **Digital Connectivity:** Jio 5G high-speed mobile data, JioFiber broadband, JioAirFiber (Fixed Wireless Access), and digital applications (JioCinema, JioTV).
*   **Solar and Battery Systems:** Heterojunction technology (HJT) high-efficiency solar modules and lithium-ion storage batteries developed at Jamnagar.

## Financial highlights
Consolidated and segment-wise financials for the full financial year ended March 31, 2026, show steady operating scale:
*   **Annual Consolidated Revenue:** ₹11,75,919 Crore (up 9.8% year-on-year), making RIL the first Indian corporate to surpass the $120 billion revenue mark.
*   **Annual Consolidated EBITDA:** Reached a record ₹2,07,911 Crore, marking a 13.4% increase year-on-year.
*   **Annual Consolidated Net Profit (PAT):** Stood at ₹95,610 Crore, up 18.3% compared to the previous fiscal year.
*   **Capital Expenditure:** Total annual CAPEX was ₹1,44,271 Crore, dedicated to 5G coverage densification, retail store expansion, and new energy infrastructure.
*   **Net Debt:** Managed at ₹1,24,717 Crore, providing a comfortable leverage ratio relative to annual EBITDA.
*   **Dividend:** The Board recommended a final dividend of ₹6.00 per equity share for FY26.

## Competitive advantages
*   **Scale and Operational Integration:** Owns and operates the world’s largest single-site refinery, allowing RIL to adjust production between fuels and chemical building blocks based on global market spreads.
*   **Leading Telecom Platform:** Jio Platforms commands a massive subscriber base of over 524 million, serving as India's largest carrier with a leading position in 5G mobile traffic and digital enterprise services.
*   **Retail Network Reach:** Reliance Retail operates over 20,160 stores covering 78.3 million square feet of retail space, offering scale and a secure supply chain that traditional retailers cannot easily match.
*   **Integrated Clean Energy Giga Complex:** A 5,000-acre integrated manufacturing ecosystem in Jamnagar lowers production costs for solar modules, batteries, and hydrogen through integrated production processes.

## Risk factors
*   **Geopolitical and Macro Volatility:** Refining and petrochemical spreads are highly sensitive to geopolitical shifts, crude oil pricing dynamics, and global demand slowdowns.
*   **Significant Capital Commitments:** Scaling the New Energy business and deepwater gas exploration requires massive capital expenditure, which could impact free cash flow generation if project timelines are delayed.
*   **Intense Market Competition:** Faced with active competition across the retail, e-commerce, and digital platforms segments, which requires ongoing promotions and pricing adjustments.
*   **Regulatory Changes:** Changes in natural gas price caps, domestic hydrocarbon taxes, or data privacy laws could impact operational cash flows.

## Management outlook
RIL's management, led by Chairman Mukesh D. Ambani, is focused on executing three strategic priorities over the medium term. First, the group is preparing for the public listing of its digital arm, Jio Platforms, with a draft red herring prospectus (DRHP) planned for submission in mid-2026. Second, the company is systematically commissioning integrated manufacturing lines at the Dhirubhai Ambani Green Energy Giga Complex, targeting 10 GW of annual solar module capacity. Third, the group is embedding artificial intelligence (AI) across its media, entertainment, and retail divisions to improve demand forecasting and customer personalization. Management remains committed to achieving net-zero carbon operations across all business units by 2035.
