## Company overview
State Bank of India (SBI) is an Indian multinational, public sector banking and financial services statutory body. Headquartered in Mumbai, Maharashtra, SBI is the largest public sector bank in India, holding an asset market share of over 23% alongside a quarter of the total deposits and advances market share. Tracing its lineage back to the Bank of Calcutta in 1806, the bank evolved into the Imperial Bank of India before being nationalized as the State Bank of India in 1955. Today, SBI operates a vast physical network of more than 22,500 branches, over 65,000 ATMs, and several international offices in more than 29 countries. Under the leadership of Chairman Challa Sreenivasulu Setty, the bank manages a massive balance sheet with total business crossing ₹109 Lakh Crore.

## Business segments
*   **Retail and Digital Banking:** The largest division of the bank, offering home loans, auto loans, personal financing, deposits, savings accounts, and digital banking services. This segment is supported by the proprietary digital platform YONO (You Only Need One).
*   **Corporate and Wholesale Banking:** Caters to large corporations, multinational firms, public sector undertakings, and middle-market enterprises, providing structured debt, working capital, project finance, cash management, and trade advisory services.
*   **SME and Agricultural Banking:** Direct credit lines, overdrafts, and term financing for small and medium-scale industries and agricultural businesses across semi-urban and rural areas.
*   **Treasury Operations:** Manages the bank's investment portfolio, regulatory statutory liquidity ratios (SLR), foreign exchange trading, inter-bank lending, and interest rate hedging products.
*   **Global/International Operations:** Provides overseas retail and commercial operations, foreign currency loans, export-import finance, and international remittance channels.

## Products
*   **Deposit Accounts:** Comprehensive options for retail savings, high-value corporate current accounts, salary packages, and customizable term/fixed deposits.
*   **Retail Lending:** High-volume personal asset products such as housing loans, auto financing, gold loans, educational loans, and personal overdraft facilities.
*   **Business Credit:** SME loans, corporate working capital financing, project loans, and letters of credit.
*   **Digital Offerings:** The YONO mobile app, net banking portals, secure corporate cash management solutions, and merchant payment systems.
*   **Insurance & Asset Management:** Group cross-selling of life/general insurance, pension schemes, and mutual fund products via corporate subsidiaries (such as SBI Life, SBI General, and SBI Mutual Fund).

## Financial highlights
Consolidated and standalone financial results for the full financial year ended March 31, 2026, demonstrate steady business execution:
*   **Total Business Volume:** Crossed ₹109 Lakh Crore during the fiscal year, with total deposits standing at ₹59.80 Lakh Crore and advances reaching ₹49.30 Lakh Crore.
*   **Annual Standalone Net Profit (PAT):** Achieved a record ₹80,032 Crore, registering a year-on-year growth of 12.88% compared to ₹70,901 Crore in FY25.
*   **Operating Profit:** Grew by 11.25% year-on-year to ₹1,23,015 Crore.
*   **Whole Bank Net Interest Margin (NIM):** Maintained at 2.91% for the full year (with domestic NIM standing at 3.03%).
*   **Return Metrics:** Return on Assets (ROA) stood at 1.12%, while Return on Equity (ROE) was reported at 18.57%.
*   **Dividend:** The board declared a final equity share dividend of ₹17.35 per share for the fiscal year.

## Competitive advantages
*   **Unrivaled Distribution Network:** The bank's branch and ATM network reaches rural and semi-urban populations, giving it a stable and cost-efficient liability base.
*   **Dominant Digital Footprint:** The YONO platform is a major asset; in FY26, more than 66% of SBI savings accounts were opened digitally through YONO, which handles high digital transaction volumes.
*   **Strong Sovereign Support:** As India's premier public sector bank, SBI maintains high credit ratings and strong depositor trust, supported by its status as a systemically important domestic bank (D-SIB).
*   **Subsidiary Synergy:** The bank’s market-leading subsidiaries in life insurance, card payments, investment banking, and mutual funds provide a diversified source of fee-income streams.

## Risk factors
*   **Net Interest Margin Compression:** Intense competition for retail deposits and rising deposit rates have increased the cost of funds, contributing to margin contraction.
*   **Corporate Credit and Treasury Exposure:** Subject to credit concentration risks in large infrastructure loans and treasury mark-to-market variations during shifting interest rate environments.
*   **SME and Agri Delinquencies:** Unsecured or cash-flow sensitive SME loans and agricultural credit remain vulnerable to climatic variations and regional economic disruptions.
*   **Technology and Security Threats:** Managing high transaction volumes on alternate channels (which handle 98.7% of total bank transactions) requires continuous investment to mitigate technical failures and cybersecurity risks.

## Management outlook
State Bank of India’s management, under Chairman CS Setty, remains cautiously optimistic about credit growth, targeting a loan book expansion of 13% to 15% for the upcoming fiscal year. In terms of resource mobilization, the bank is prioritizing granular retail term deposits and improving CASA (Current Account Savings Account) ratios to address rising funding costs. Management is also focused on risk-managed portfolio diversification, particularly expanding SME and high-yield retail personal loans, while continuing to invest in technology to upgrade the YONO platform and build advanced cybersecurity defense tools.
