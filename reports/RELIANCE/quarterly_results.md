## Revenue
For the fourth quarter (Q4) ended March 31, 2026, Reliance Industries reported a consolidated gross revenue of ₹3,25,290 Crore, up 12.9% year-on-year compared to ₹2,88,138 Crore in Q4 FY25. Consolidated revenue from operations rose 13.0% year-on-year to ₹2,98,621 Crore, supported by steady demand in retail, digital services, and O2C segments.

## PAT
The company's consolidated Profit After Tax (PAT) stood at ₹20,616 Crore for Q4 FY26, down 8.1% compared to ₹22,434 Crore in the corresponding quarter of the previous fiscal year. 
*   **Net Profit Attributable to Owners:** Stood at ₹16,971 Crore, representing a decline of 12.6% year-on-year from ₹19,407 Crore, and a sequential decline of 8.98% compared to Q3 FY26.
*   **Factors Impacting Profitability:** The profitability decline was driven by lower refining spreads in the O2C segment due to global supply chain disruptions, combined with higher tax expenses and an increase in finance costs.

## EPS
For the quarter ended March 31, 2026, basic Earnings Per Share (EPS) was ₹12.54, compared to ₹14.34 in the corresponding period of the previous fiscal year.

## Key operational highlights
*   **Jio Platforms Performance:** Delivered a strong quarter with EBITDA growing 17.9% year-on-year to ₹20,060 Crore. The total mobile subscriber base reached 524 million, with 268 million customers migrated to the Jio True5G network.
*   **Reliance Retail Momentum:** Gross revenue for the retail segment rose 11.0% year-on-year to ₹98,232 Crore, while quarterly EBITDA grew 3.1% to ₹6,921 Crore. The retail unit opened 333 new stores during the quarter, bringing its total store network to 20,160.
*   **Oil-to-Chemicals (O2C):** Steady production and domestic fuel sales offset lower refining margins caused by global trade disruptions, maintaining high capacity utilization across the Jamnagar refining complex.
*   **New Energy Business Milestone:** The company delivered its first 200 megawatt-peak (MWp) batch of high-efficiency heterojunction technology (HJT) solar modules from its green energy complex in Jamnagar, marking the operational launch of its clean energy manufacturing division.
*   **Quarterly Capital Expenditure:** Capital expenditure for the quarter stood at ₹40,560 Crore, focused on retail store expansion, fiber rollout, and solar cell manufacturing equipment.

## Management commentary
Mukesh D. Ambani, Chairman and Managing Director, commented on the quarterly results:

"Our performance through FY26 shows the resilience of our integrated business model. During the year, we faced geopolitical disruptions, volatile energy prices, and shifting global trade patterns that weighed on businesses worldwide. 

India held its economic growth course through all this, as did Reliance. The breadth of our portfolio and our strong domestic focus helped us navigate volatility in the external environment. Our consumer businesses continued to show steady momentum, with Jio’s subscriber base crossing 524 million and Retail surpassing 20,000 stores. 

We also reached a key milestone in our New Energy business, delivering our first batch of high-efficiency solar modules from Jamnagar. While global margins in our traditional energy segment faced near-term headwinds, our focus remains on scaling our digital, retail, and green energy platforms to drive sustainable growth."

## Future guidance
*   **Capital Allocation and Listings:** RIL is on track to file its draft red herring prospectus (DRHP) for the public listing of Jio Platforms, aiming to complete the initial public offering (IPO) by late 2026.
*   **New Energy Capacity Scaling:** The company expects to scale its solar module manufacturing capacity to 10 gigawatt-peak (GWp) annually in the next phase, progressively commissioning integrated silicon, ingot, and wafer production lines.
*   **Digital and Satellite Services:** Jio intends to scale its satellite-based gigabit broadband services and enter new enterprise partnerships for private 5G networks and cloud computing.
*   **Retail Growth Strategy:** Reliance Retail will continue to prioritize its digital commerce and hyper-local delivery networks, aiming to improve operating margins in the next fiscal year.
