## Revenue
For the fourth quarter (Q4) of the fiscal year ended March 31, 2026, Bajaj Finance reported a consolidated Net Interest Income (NII) of ₹11,781 Crore. This represents a 20.1% growth compared to the ₹9,807 Crore reported in Q4 FY25, supported by steady loan additions and growth in interest-earning assets.
*   **Total Standalone Income:** Rose 17.7% year-on-year to ₹18,430.64 Crore, compared to ₹15,649.91 Crore in the corresponding period a year ago.
*   **Pre-Provision Operating Profit (PPOP):** Surged 20.5% year-on-year to ₹9,417.40 Crore.

## PAT
Consolidated Profit After Tax (PAT) for Q4 FY26 stood at ₹5,465 Crore, marking a 22.0% increase compared to ₹4,479.57 Crore in the fourth quarter of the previous fiscal year. On a sequential basis, consolidated net profit grew 37.38% from the ₹3,977.85 Crore reported in Q3 FY26, helped by lower quarterly credit provisioning and disciplined operating expenses.

## EPS
For the quarter ended March 31, 2026, consolidated basic Earnings Per Share (EPS) reached ₹88.86 per share, up from the corresponding period's EPS of ₹72.83. Standalone basic EPS for the quarter was reported at ₹78.69 per share.

## Key operational highlights
*   **AUM Milestone:** Consolidated Assets Under Management crossed ₹5.09 Lakh Crore, standing at ₹5,09,980 Crore, a growth of 22.4% year-on-year.
*   **Customer Franchise:** Added 3.93 million new customers during the quarter, taking the total customer base to 119.33 million.
*   **Loan Additions:** Booked 12.89 million new loans during Q4 FY26, a 20.5% growth compared to 10.70 million loans in Q4 FY25.
*   **Asset Quality Improvement:** Gross NPA (GNPA) improved sequentially to 1.01% from 1.2% in Q3 FY26, while Net NPA (NNPA) improved to 0.41% from 0.5% in the previous quarter. Provision coverage ratio stood at 60%.
*   **Loan Losses & Provisions:** Stood at ₹2,008 Crore for Q4, down from ₹2,167 Crore a year ago as the company moderated loan volumes to higher-risk segments.

## Management commentary
Rajeev Jain, Vice Chairman and Managing Director, shared his perspective during the earnings call:

"Q4 of FY26 was a strong quarter across all key operational metrics, including volume growth, customer acquisition, and AUM. Our consolidated AUM grew by 22% to cross the ₹5 lakh crore mark. This growth was driven by steady consumer demand, along with strong performance in our mortgage and urban sales finance divisions.

We took steps to manage risks in our MSME book, slowing down segment growth to 6% due to industry-wide credit stress. However, our newer verticals, including gold loans and microfinance, are seeing good traction. Asset quality has remained stable, with GNPA improving sequentially to 1.01%. Our focus going forward is to continue leveraging 'Consumer AI' to improve personalization and cross-selling, while keeping credit costs well managed."

## Future guidance
*   **AUM Growth:** The company targets consolidated AUM growth of 21% to 23% for the next fiscal year, balancing credit growth with margin stability.
*   **Credit Cost:** BFL has guided for a consolidated credit cost of 1.45% to 1.60% for FY27, backed by stable asset quality and cautious lending parameters.
*   **Operating Costs:** The company plans to manage its Opex-to-Net Total Income (NTI) ratio between 33% and 35%, leveraging automated digital cross-sell processes to improve operational efficiency.
*   **Returns Guidance:** BFL aims to maintain a consolidated ROA profile of 4.3%–4.7% and a consolidated ROE of 19%–21% over the medium term.
