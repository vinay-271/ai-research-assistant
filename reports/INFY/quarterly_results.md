## Revenue
For the fourth quarter (Q4) of the fiscal year ended March 31, 2026, Infosys reported a consolidated revenue from operations of ₹46,402 Crore. This represents a 13.4% year-on-year growth compared to the ₹40,925 Crore reported in Q4 FY25. On a sequential basis, revenue grew by 2.0% in rupee terms. 
*   **Constant Currency Growth:** Revenues in constant currency grew by 4.1% year-on-year, while declining by 1.3% sequentially compared to Q3 FY26, reflecting typical post-holiday seasonal patterns and project wrap-ups.
*   **USD Revenue:** Standalone Q4 revenues stood at $5,040 million, up 6.6% year-on-year.

## PAT
Consolidated Net Profit (after non-controlling interests) for Q4 FY26 stood at ₹8,501 Crore, showing a 20.9% growth year-on-year compared to ₹7,033 Crore in Q4 FY25. Sequentially, consolidated net profit increased by 27.8% from Q3 FY26's net profit of ₹6,654 Crore, primarily aided by lower quarterly tax provisions and a reversal of tax reserves amounting to ₹774 Crore under income tax assessment orders.

## EPS
For the quarter ended March 31, 2026, standalone basic and diluted Earnings Per Share (EPS) stood at ₹21.01 per share, representing an increase of 23.8% year-on-year and 29.9% sequentially.

## Key operational highlights
*   **Operating Margin Expansion:** Reported Q4 operating margin expanded to 21.0%, registering a sequential improvement of 2.6% over Q3 FY26 (which had been heavily impacted by one-time labor and gratuity provisions).
*   **Large Deal TCV:** Secured large deal wins of $3.2 billion during the quarter.
*   **Strong Cash Generation:** Free cash flow (FCF) for the quarter was robust at ₹7,711 Crore ($919 million).
*   **Capital Returns:** The Board declared a final dividend of ₹25 per equity share, with June 10, 2026 set as the record date and June 25, 2026 as the payment date.
*   **Hiring Program:** The company announced a targeting program to hire approximately 20,000 freshers during the next financial year to support project deployment.

## Management commentary
Salil Parekh, CEO and MD of Infosys, shared his perspective during the media briefing:

"We delivered a resilient performance in Q4 and full-year FY26, with revenue crossing the $20 billion mark, registering a 3.1% growth in constant currency. Our large deal wins of $14.9 billion for the year reflect the strength of our enterprise AI value proposition and market share gains in large transformation opportunities. 

Our AI-first services strategy across six areas is gaining traction, further strengthened by ecosystem partnerships that allow clients to unlock value at scale. The Topaz and Cobalt platforms are helping us build client trust and expand our market presence. Operating margins improved to 21.0% this quarter due to cost discipline, and our healthy cash generation allows us to declare a final dividend of ₹25 per share. We will continue focusing on AI-led automation and delivery quality as we enter the next fiscal year."

## Future guidance
*   **FY27 Revenue Guidance:** The company has guided for constant currency revenue growth of 1.5% to 3.5% for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2027.
*   **FY27 Operating Margin:** Operating margin guidance for the next fiscal year has been set at 20% to 22%, reflecting continuous automation and cost-control initiatives.
*   **Utilization and Cost Optimization:** Management plans to prioritize the reduction of subcontractor usage and leverage its bench pool to improve billable utilization over the upcoming quarters.
