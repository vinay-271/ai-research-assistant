## Revenue
For the fourth quarter (Q4) of the financial year ended March 31, 2026, ITC reported a consolidated revenue from operations of ₹23,821.48 Crore. This represents a 16.91% growth year-on-year compared to the ₹20,376.36 Crore reported in Q4 FY25, and a 9.74% sequential increase compared to Q3 FY26. 
*   **Gross Revenue Comparability:** The revenue growth was partly driven by a significant reclassification of excise duty on cigarettes following a change in the taxation structure effective February 1, 2026, making year-on-year comparisons of gross numbers not strictly comparable.

## PAT
ITC's consolidated net profit for Q4 FY26 stood at ₹5,387.97 Crore, down 72.3% year-on-year from ₹19,727.30 Crore in Q4 FY25. 
*   **Base Effect Explanation:** The sharp decline was due to a high base in Q4 FY25, which included a one-time exceptional gain of ₹15,179 Crore related to the demerger of the hotels business.
*   **Continuing Operations Profitability:** Adjusting for exceptional items, standalone profit after tax from continuing operations rose by 6.1% to ₹5,470 Crore, up from ₹5,155 Crore in Q4 FY25, demonstrating steady underlying growth.

## EPS
For the quarter ended March 31, 2026, standalone basic Earnings Per Share (EPS) stood at ₹4.35 per share, compared to the previous year’s reported EPS of ₹15.82 (which was inflated by the demerger gain). For the full fiscal year ended March 31, 2026, basic EPS reached ₹16.20.

## Key operational highlights
*   **Cigarettes Segment:** Segment revenue stood at ₹11,951.72 Crore, up from ₹9,228.66 Crore in Q4 FY25. This increase reflects the revised excise tax structures and steady volumes in premium filters.
*   **FMCG-Others Segment:** Segment revenue rose to ₹6,352.41 Crore. The division achieved a 200 bps year-on-year expansion in EBITDA margins (excluding Sresta) to reach 11.0%, supported by premiumization and supply chain efficiencies.
*   **Agri-Business Segment:** Agri-business recorded resilient commodity volumes, led by leaf tobacco exports, despite challenges from the West Asia shipping disruptions.
*   **Paperboards, Paper & Packaging Segment:** Operated in a challenging domestic market due to low-priced Chinese and Indonesian packaging board supplies, combined with elevated wood costs.
*   **Quarterly EBITDA:** Standalone EBITDA grew by 7.3% year-on-year to ₹6,425 Crore.

## Management commentary
Sanjiv Puri, Chairman and Managing Director, shared his perspective during the earnings call:

"Our performance during the fourth quarter of FY26 reflects the strength of our businesses in a challenging macroeconomic environment. Gross consolidated revenue grew by 16.9% to ₹23,821 crore, supported by steady volumes in our cigarette business and positive momentum in FMCG-Others. 

While the reported net profit decline on a year-on-year basis looks steep, it is entirely a reflection of the high base from last year's hotels demerger exceptional gain of over ₹15,000 crore. If you look at our core operations, we achieved steady growth. Our FMCG-Others margins expanded by 200 basis points to reach 11%, driven by our premium portfolio, while brands like Yogabar, Mother Sparsh, and Prasuma continue to gain traction. We will continue to focus on cost management and product premiumization to drive sustainable growth."

## Future guidance
*   **FMCG margin expansion:** The company aims to sustain its margin expansion trajectory in FMCG-Others, targeting a 100-150 bps improvement over the medium term.
*   **Capital Expenditure:** Management plans to maintain a disciplined CAPEX strategy, directing capital toward packaging innovations, digital technology, and capacity additions in food processing.
*   **Cigarette Volumes:** ITC expects cigarette volumes to stay range-bound, focusing on product innovation and premium filters to manage the impact of the February 2026 excise tax hikes.
*   **Dividend Payout:** The company expects to maintain its high dividend payout ratio, returning surplus cash to shareholders.
