## Company overview
ITC Limited, established in 1910 as the Imperial Tobacco Company of India Limited, is one of India's preeminent private sector conglomerates. Headquartered in Kolkata, West Bengal, the company has evolved from a pure-play tobacco manufacturer into a highly diversified enterprise with business interests spanning Fast-Moving Consumer Goods (FMCG), Agri-Business, and Paperboards, Paper & Packaging. In the previous fiscal year (FY25), the company completed the demerger of its premier Hotels division into a separate listed entity to unlock shareholder value. Led by Chairman and Managing Director Sanjiv Puri, ITC employs thousands of people across India and operates on the "ITC Next" strategic framework, focusing on building structural competitive advantages, digital acceleration, and sustainability.

## Business segments
*   **FMCG-Cigarettes:** The primary cash-generating engine of the company, holding a dominant market leadership position in the organized Indian cigarette market.
*   **FMCG-Others:** A high-growth segment containing a portfolio of branded packaged foods, personal care products, education and stationery, safety matches, and agarbattis (incense sticks).
*   **Agri-Business:** Focuses on agricultural commodity sourcing and trading, leaf tobacco exports, and crop-value-chain enablement, heavily supported by the digital "e-Choupal" and "ITC MAARS" farmer network platforms.
*   **Paperboards, Paper & Packaging:** Provides eco-friendly packaging, specialty papers, and high-quality paperboards to pharmaceutical, publishing, food service, and consumer goods industries.

## Financial highlights
Consolidated and standalone financial parameters for the full financial year ended March 31, 2026, indicate a stable growth trajectory:
*   **Consolidated Annual Revenue:** ₹89,913.33 Crore, marking a 10.1% growth year-on-year compared to ₹81,612.78 Crore in FY25.
*   **Gross Revenue (Standalone):** Reached ₹80,867.49 Crore, up 10.1% YoY.
*   **Annual EBITDA:** Expanded by 4.9% to ₹25,208.22 Crore, driven by steady FMCG-Others margins.
*   **Profit Before Exceptional Items and Tax:** Stood at ₹26,951.47 Crore for the fiscal year.
*   **Annual EPS:** Reported at ₹16.20 per share (compared to ₹16.07 in the previous fiscal year).
*   **Dividend Payout:** Recommended a final dividend of ₹8.00 per share, which, together with the interim dividend of ₹6.50 per share, brings the total dividend for FY26 to ₹14.50 per share.

## Competitive advantages
*   **Vast Distribution Rebound:** Reaches over 7 million retail touchpoints across India, bypassing conventional wholesale networks through direct-to-retail delivery systems.
*   **Backward Agri-Sourcing Synergies:** The Agri-Business segment directly sources wheat, potato, and dairy for foods like Aashirvaad flour, Bingo! chips, and Sunfeast biscuits, optimizing the overall raw material supply chain.
*   **Strong Cash Engine:** The high-margin cigarette business generates consistent, strong cash flows, enabling ITC to invest in its FMCG-Others brand-building campaigns and capital-intensive packaging technologies.
*   **Robust Brand Equity:** Holds top-tier market share positions in Indian staples (Aashirvaad), cream biscuits (Sunfeast), bridge snacks (Bingo!), instant noodles (YiPPee!), and student notebooks (Classmate).

## Risk factors
*   **Regulatory & Tax Pressures on Cigarettes:** Subject to unpredictable tax structural changes, such as the major excise revisions implemented in February 2026, which can lead to near-term volume adjustments.
*   **Commodity Cost Inflation:** Vulnerable to price hikes in key agricultural and packaging raw materials, including pulpwood, wheat, edible oils, and soap noodles.
*   **Fierce FMCG Competition:** Competes directly with established multinational corporations and agile direct-to-consumer (D2C) brands across personal care and packaged food segments.
*   **Geopolitical and Export Restrictions:** Agri-business exports are vulnerable to international shipping disruptions and domestic export bans (e.g., on select food staples).

## Management outlook
Under the "ITC Next" strategy, management plans to focus on structural cost management, volume-led growth, and continuous premiumization. The company is prioritizing margin expansion in the FMCG-Others segment, aiming for incremental improvement through digital execution and localized sourcing. In the Paperboards division, the priority is addressing low-priced Chinese imports by focusing on sustainable, value-added products. Following the successful hotels spin-off, the management team remains focused on capital allocation and maintaining a high dividend payout ratio for shareholders.
