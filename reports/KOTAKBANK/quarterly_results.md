## Revenue
For the fourth quarter (Q4) of the fiscal year ended March 31, 2026, Kotak Mahindra Bank reported stable core and non-fund-based income streams:
*   **Net Interest Income (NII):** Standalone NII grew 8.1% year-on-year to ₹7,876 Crore, up from ₹7,284 Crore in Q4 FY25. Sequentially, NII grew 4.1% from ₹7,565 Crore in Q3 FY26.
*   **Other Income:** Non-interest income was ₹3,116 Crore, down 2.1% compared to ₹3,184 Crore in the corresponding quarter of the previous fiscal year.
*   **Fees and Services:** Rose 6.0% year-on-year to ₹2,767 Crore, supported by steady retail transaction fee momentum.

## PAT
The bank reported a standalone Profit After Tax (PAT) of ₹4,027 Crore for Q4 FY26, up 13.4% compared to ₹3,552 Crore in Q4 FY25. Sequentially, standalone net profit rose 17.0% from Q3 FY26's PAT of ₹3,446 Crore, driven by lower quarterly provisions and improving asset quality. Consolidated PAT for the quarter stood at ₹5,423 Crore, up 10.0% year-on-year.

## EPS
*   **Stock Split Alignment:** Book value per share stood at ₹182, computed based on the stock sub-division (split) of one equity share of face value ₹5 into five equity shares of face value ₹1, effective January 14, 2026.
*   **Earnings Per Share:** Standalone basic EPS for the quarter was ₹2.03 on the split-adjusted face value of ₹1 per share. Consolidated basic EPS reached ₹2.73 for the quarter.

## Key operational highlights
*   **Balanced Credit and Liability Growth:** Standalone net advances stood at ₹4.96 Lakh Crore, growing 16.0% year-on-year, while total deposits grew 15.0% to ₹5.72 Lakh Crore, keeping credit-to-deposit ratios in check.
*   **CASA Stability:** Current Account Savings Account (CASA) ratio stood at 43.3% as of March 31, 2026.
*   **Net Interest Margin (NIM):** Standalone NIM stood at 4.67% for the quarter, compared to 4.97% in Q4 FY25 and 4.54% in Q3 FY26.
*   **Significant Decline in Provisions:** Provisions dropped 43.2% year-on-year and 36.3% sequentially to ₹516 Crore. The annualized credit cost for the quarter was managed at 0.39%.
*   **Asset Quality Improvement:** Standalone Gross NPA improved sequentially to 1.20% from 1.30% in Q3 FY26, while Net NPA declined by 6 basis points sequentially to 0.25%.
*   **Proposed Dividend:** The Board proposed a final dividend of ₹0.65 per share having a face value of ₹1 for the fiscal year ended March 31, 2026, subject to shareholder approval.

## Management commentary
Ashok Vaswani, Managing Director and CEO, commented during the earnings call:

"We closed the financial year on a stable note, with Q4 standalone net profit growing 13% to ₹4,027 crore, supported by clean credit metrics and stable liability gathering. NII rose 8% to ₹7,876 crore while we managed our asset quality, bringing our GNPA down to 1.20% and keeping our credit costs annualized at 0.39% for the quarter.

Our loan book grew 16% and deposits rose 15%, showing balanced growth. Our CASA ratio has remained resilient at 43.3%, which supports our overall cost of funds. Pursuant to regulatory guidelines, we are moving ahead with group simplification, bringing the lending activities of Kotak Mahindra Investments Limited departmentally within the bank from April 1, 2026. Our focus is to deploy our surplus capital through both organic and inorganic channels while building a leaner and more tech-enabled bank."

## Future guidance
*   **NIM Outlook:** Management expects a gradual, mild reduction in Net Interest Margin in FY27 due to competitive retail deposit pricing, but the pace of contraction is expected to slow compared to FY26.
*   **Capital Deployment and M&A:** Given its high capital adequacy of 23.0%, the bank is actively evaluating organic and inorganic growth opportunities, including potential retail loan portfolios and financial infrastructure assets, to support returns on capital.
*   **SME and Corporate Advances:** Management expects corporate and SME credit segments to remain growth drivers, targeting credit growth in the mid-teens for the next fiscal year.
*   **Operating Leverage:** The bank intends to manage its Cost-to-Assets ratio close to its current baseline of 2.75% while continuing to make necessary IT infrastructure investments.
