## Revenue
For the fourth quarter (Q4) ended March 31, 2026, ICICI Bank reported a total standalone income of ₹50,584.38 Crore, compared to ₹49,690.87 Crore in the corresponding quarter of the previous fiscal year.
*   **Net Interest Income (NII):** Grew 8.4% year-on-year to ₹22,979 Crore, up from ₹21,193 Crore in Q4 FY25.
*   **Non-Interest Income:** Excluding treasury, non-interest income rose 5.6% year-on-year to ₹7,415 Crore, supported by retail and corporate transaction fees.
*   **Treasury Book:** Registered a treasury loss of ₹106 Crore for the quarter, compared to a gain of ₹239 Crore in Q4 FY25, reflecting market movements and the reduction of onshore open positions.

## PAT
ICICI Bank posted a standalone Net Profit After Tax (PAT) of ₹13,701.68 Crore for Q4 FY26, marking an 8.5% year-on-year increase from the ₹12,629.58 Crore reported in Q4 FY25. Sequentially, standalone PAT rose 21.1% compared to ₹11,318 Crore in Q3 FY26, driven by a sharp decline in quarterly credit provisioning.

## EPS
For the quarter ended March 31, 2026, standalone basic and diluted Earnings Per Share (EPS) was ₹19.15 per share, up from ₹17.87 in the corresponding quarter of the previous fiscal year.

## Key operational highlights
*   **Loan Expansion:** Total loan portfolio grew 15.8% year-on-year to stand at ₹15,53,893 Crore at March 31, 2026. Domestic advances grew by 11.5% sequentially during the half-year period.
*   **Deposit Mobilization:** Total period-end deposits grew 11.4% year-on-year to ₹17,94,625 Crore. Average Current Account and Savings Account (CASA) ratio stood at 38.6% for Q4 FY26.
*   **Operating Margins:** Net Interest Margin (NIM) stood at 4.32% in Q4 FY26, compared to 4.30% in Q3 FY26 and 4.32% for the full year FY26. Core operating profit rose 5.1% YoY to ₹18,305 Crore.
*   **Asset Quality Metrics:** Gross NPA ratio improved to 1.40% as of March 31, 2026, down from 1.53% in Q3 FY26 and 1.67% in Q4 FY25. Net NPA stood at 0.33%, down from 0.39% in the prior year.
*   **Sharp Decline in Provisions:** Quarterly provisions fell 89.2% year-on-year to ₹96.16 Crore, compared to ₹890.70 Crore in Q4 FY25. The bank continues to hold a contingency provision of ₹13,100 Crore.
*   **Shareholder Dividend:** The Board recommended a final dividend of ₹12 per equity share for the full financial year 2025-26.

## Management commentary
Sandeep Batra, Executive Director, shared his perspective during the media call following the results:

"Our performance during Q4 of FY26 indicates stable progress across our retail and corporate lending portfolios. Standalone net profit grew by 8.5% to ₹13,702 crore, supported by healthy net interest income of ₹22,979 crore and consistent cost discipline. 

One of the key drivers of our profit growth this quarter was a sharp reduction in our provisions, which fell to ₹96 crore, reflecting our stable asset quality and healthy recoveries. While our treasury book saw a minor loss of ₹106 crore due to open position adjustments under regulatory guidelines, our core operating margins remained stable at 4.32%. We are continuing to invest in our digital capabilities, including biometric authorizations for UPI, to improve our customer experience and transactional safety."

## Future guidance
*   **Loan and Deposit Growth:** The bank plans to continue growing its retail and corporate loan portfolios selectively, aiming to align asset growth with granular retail deposit mobilization.
*   **NIM Stability:** Management expects NIMs to remain relatively stable within the 4.25% to 4.35% range, focusing on deposit pricing management.
*   **Credit Costs:** The bank intends to maintain low credit costs, supported by contingency buffers and focused recoveries in retail and corporate loan books.
*   **Operating Expenses:** The bank will continue to invest in branch expansion and technology infrastructure while seeking operational efficiencies to manage the overall cost-to-income ratio.
