## Revenue
For the fourth quarter (Q4) ended March 31, 2026, NTPC reported a consolidated revenue from operations of ₹49,698 Crore. This remains largely flat compared to the ₹49,833 Crore reported in the corresponding quarter of the previous fiscal year.
*   **Standalone Operational Revenue:** Stood at ₹43,111 Crore, slightly higher by 6% compared to ₹40,644 Crore in Q4 FY25, but down 1.8% sequentially from Q3 FY26's revenue of ₹43,904 Crore, reflecting a seasonal shift in thermal power dispatch.

## PAT
The state-run power major reported a consolidated Profit After Tax (PAT) of ₹10,615 Crore for Q4 FY26, marking a 34.4% year-on-year jump from the ₹7,897 Crore reported in Q4 FY25.
*   **Standalone PAT:** Stood at ₹8,747 Crore for the quarter, up 75.4% sequentially from Q3 FY26's net profit of ₹4,987 Crore, and up 51.4% compared to the ₹5,778 Crore reported in Q4 FY25. This bottom-line performance was driven by better fuel management, operational efficiencies, and lower finance costs.

## EPS
For the quarter ended March 31, 2026, standalone basic Earnings Per Share (EPS) rose to ₹9.02 per share, up from the sequential quarter's basic EPS of ₹5.14. For the full year FY26, standalone adjusted basic EPS reached ₹20.10.

## Key operational highlights
*   **Capacity Addition Record:** NTPC Group added 9,618 MW of capacity in FY26, bringing its total installed capacity base to 89,108 MW (up from 79,930 MW in FY25). This represents the highest annual capacity addition in the company's history.
*   **Renewable Additions:** Renewable energy projects contributed 5,488 MW to the total annual additions, bringing the group's total operational renewable capacity close to 12,068 MW.
*   **Coal Plant Load Factor (PLF):** Standing out against industry benchmarks, NTPC's standalone coal plants achieved a PLF of 76.16% during Q4 FY26, significantly higher than the average national coal PLF of 63.20%.
*   **Generation Velocity:** Commercial generation rose 4.4% quarter-on-quarter to 91.05 billion units during the fourth quarter.
*   **Clean Energy Co-firing:** In line with emission reduction goals, NTPC Group plants co-fired 15.19 lakh metric tonnes of biomass in FY26, more than double the volume used in the previous fiscal year.

## Management commentary
CMD Gurdeep Singh provided the following perspective during the earnings call:

"Our results for the fourth quarter and full fiscal year 2026 demonstrate our strong operational execution and capital management. On a standalone basis, our net profit grew by 18% to ₹23,162 crore for the full year, while consolidated quarterly profit grew by 34.4% to ₹10,615 crore. 

This financial performance has been supported by a record capacity addition of 9,618 MW, which includes 5,488 MW of renewable energy. Our coal plants achieved a PLF of 72.04% for the full year, continuing to outperform the national average. While we maintain a robust under-construction pipeline of 34,188 MW, we are also making progress on our clean energy initiatives. This includes the launch of major solar tenders and the commencement of early construction activities at our Mahi Banswara nuclear project, positioning us to support India's growing electricity demand."

## Future guidance
*   **Annual Capacity Addition Targets:** The company has targeted annual capacity additions of approximately 9,557 MW in FY27 (comprising 1,070 MW thermal, 250 MW hydro, and 8,237 MW renewable) and 10,039 MW in FY28 (comprising 1,460 MW thermal, 444 MW hydro, and 8,135 MW renewable).
*   **Substantial Capital Outlay:** CapEx guidance for its subsidiary NGEL has been projected at ₹35,800 Crore for FY27, ₹56,000 Crore for FY28, and ₹48,000 Crore for FY29 to support its solar and wind targets.
*   **Nuclear Baseload Goals:** The Mahi Banswara nuclear project (2.8 GW, four units of 700 MW) is progressing on schedule, with the first concrete pour planned for August 2027 and synchronization of Unit 1 targeted for November 2032.
*   **Battery Storage Expansion:** To optimize renewable generation, the company is planning a 14.3 GWh Battery Energy Storage System (BESS) development program over the next six years.
