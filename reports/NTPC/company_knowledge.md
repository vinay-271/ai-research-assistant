## Company overview
NTPC Limited (formerly National Thermal Power Corporation Limited) is India's largest integrated power generation utility. Established in 1975 and headquartered in New Delhi, NTPC is a Maharatna Central Public Sector Enterprise (CPSE) under the Ministry of Power, Government of India. The company plays a critical role in India’s power landscape, historically serving as the backbone of the country's coal-fired thermal power baseload. Over the years, NTPC has evolved into a diversified energy conglomerate with operations spanning thermal coal, gas, hydro, nuclear, and utility-scale renewable energy (solar and wind). As of May 2026, the NTPC Group’s total installed capacity stands at 89,108 MW, with plans to reach 130 GW of capacity by 2032, of which 60 GW will be sourced from renewable resources.

## Business segments
*   **Generation of Electricity (Thermal, Hydro, and Nuclear):** The core segment of the business. NTPC owns, operates, and maintains coal-fired, gas-based, and hydroelectric power plants, supplying bulk electricity to state-owned power distribution companies (DISCOMs) and industrial clients under long-term Power Purchase Agreements (PPAs). The nuclear division is managed through ASHVINI, a joint venture with the Nuclear Power Corporation of India Limited (NPCIL).
*   **Renewable Energy (NGEL & NREL):** Managed through its wholly owned subsidiary NTPC Green Energy Limited (NGEL) and its step-down subsidiary NTPC Renewable Energy Limited (NREL), this segment focuses on developing, constructing, and operating solar, wind, and green hydrogen projects across India.
*   **Power Trading:** Underdeveloped and spot power supply activities managed via NTPC Vidyut Vyapar Nigam (NVVN) Limited.
*   **Coal Mining:** Owns and operates domestic captive coal mines (including Pakri Barwadih, Dulanga, and Talaipalli) to feed its thermal power stations, reducing its reliance on external suppliers and fuel imports.
*   **Consultancy and Project Management:** Provides engineering, procurement, and construction (EPC) management, operations and maintenance (O&M) consulting, and power sector training to domestic and international utility developers.

## Products
*   **Thermal Power:** Baseload bulk electricity generated through pulverized coal and combined-cycle gas turbine technologies.
*   **Renewable Power:** Grid-connected solar photovoltaic (PV) power, onshore wind energy, and hybrid wind-solar systems.
*   **Hydroelectric Power:** Pumped storage and run-of-the-river power generation through hydroelectric projects.
*   **Nuclear Power:** Planned Pressurized Heavy Water Reactor (PHWR) power supply systems developed under the joint venture ASHVINI.
*   **Green Molecules & Hydrogen:** Pilot and commercial-scale green hydrogen, green ammonia, and synthetic aviation fuel (SAF) production programs.

## Financial highlights
Consolidated and standalone financial parameters for the fiscal year ended March 31, 2026, indicate solid execution and cash generation:
*   **Consolidated Net Profit (PAT):** Annual consolidated PAT rose 15.0% year-on-year to ₹27,546 Crore, compared to ₹23,953 Crore in FY25.
*   **Standalone Net Profit (PAT):** Increased 17.9% year-on-year to ₹23,162 Crore, compared to ₹19,649 Crore in the previous fiscal year. Standalone adjusted PAT stood at ₹19,530 Crore.
*   **NTPC Standalone Revenue:** Standalone revenue from operations was reported at ₹1,65,494 Crore, compared to ₹1,70,037 Crore in FY25.
*   **Annual CapEx:** NTPC Group's annual capital expenditure rose to ₹49,068 Crore, compared to ₹44,636 Crore in FY25, reflecting an expanding investment cycle.
*   **Dividend Distribution:** Recommended a total dividend of ₹9.00 per share for the full fiscal year ended March 31, 2026, maintaining a stable yield for shareholders.

## Competitive advantages
*   **Regulated Return-on-Equity (RoE) Model:** Most of NTPC's thermal capacity operates under a regulated cost-plus tariff model approved by the Central Electricity Regulatory Commission (CERC). This guarantees a post-tax RoE of 15.5% on capital investment, ensuring highly predictable and stable cash flows.
*   **Substantial Project Scale and Execution Expertise:** Holds deep, decades-long expertise in managing large-scale infrastructure projects. This allows the company to secure land, grid connectivity, and low-cost financing more efficiently than many private developers.
*   **Fuel Security through Captive Mines:** Captive coal mining operations have steadily ramped up, with production and dispatches growing annually to feed core thermal stations and hedge against domestic coal shortages.
*   **Massive Cash Accruals for Green Pivot:** Consistent cash flow from legacy thermal assets serves as a reliable funding source for green energy initiatives, enabling its subsidiary, NGEL, to expand its renewable footprint without over-leveraging its balance sheet.

## Risk factors
*   **Coal Sourcing and Environmental Demands:** Despite captive coal expansion, any unexpected shortages in domestic coal supply can increase the company’s reliance on high-cost imported coal, impacting fuel pass-through costs.
*   **Fossil Fuel Transition Regulations:** Strict global and domestic environmental regulations aimed at reducing carbon emissions may require early decommissioning or expensive modernizations of older thermal plants.
*   **High Capital Investment Demands:** An aggressive six-year capital expenditure plan of ₹6.22 Lakh Crore through FY32—with roughly half allocated to renewable energy—will require substantial debt accumulation.
*   **Off-taker Credit Quality:** The financial health of several state-owned power distribution companies (DISCOMs) remains a concern, which could lead to payment delays or renegotiation of power tariffs.

## Management outlook
NTPC's management remains committed to a balanced transition strategy, supporting baseload thermal power demands while rapidly scaling renewable energy capacities. Under CMD Gurdeep Singh—whose term concludes on July 31, 2026—the company targets a capacity addition of 10 GW annually across the FY27–FY29 period, with its renewable energy arm, NGEL, targeting ~8 GW of annual additions. To support grid stability during peak hours, the company is implementing a 14.3 GWh battery energy storage system (BESS). In addition, the joint venture ASHVINI has commenced excavation work at the 2.8 GW Mahi Banswara nuclear plant in Rajasthan, marking NTPC’s official entry into the nuclear energy segment.
