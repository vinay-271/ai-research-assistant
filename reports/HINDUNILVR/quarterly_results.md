## Revenue
For the fourth quarter (Q4) of the fiscal year ended March 31, 2026, Hindustan Unilever reported a consolidated turnover of ₹16,207 Crore, marking an 8.1% year-on-year growth compared to ₹14,955 Crore in Q4 FY25.
*   **Underlying Sales Growth (USG):** Stood at 7.0%.
*   **Underlying Volume Growth (UVG):** Reached 6.0%, representing the highest quarterly volume growth in 12 quarters, driven by a recovery in rural and semi-urban demand.

## PAT
HUL reported a consolidated Profit After Tax (PAT) of ₹3,002 Crore for Q4 FY26, up 20.9% compared to the ₹2,475 Crore reported in Q4 FY25.
*   **PAT Before Exceptional Items (PAT bei):** Stood at ₹2,711 Crore, representing a 4.0% increase year-on-year.
*   **Exceptional Items:** Headline PAT was supported by exceptional proceeds from the demerger and divestment of the company's stake in Nutritionalab Private Limited.

## EPS
For the quarter ended March 31, 2026, HUL's basic Earnings Per Share (EPS) stood at ₹12.78, up from ₹10.53 in Q4 FY25. Standalone basic EPS before exceptional items was reported at ₹11.53.

## Key operational highlights
*   **Home Care Performance:** The segment grew 9.0% year-on-year, marking its highest growth in 11 quarters. Fabric Wash delivered double-digit growth, and the liquids portfolio continued to show strong momentum.
*   **Beauty & Wellbeing Performance:** Recorded 8.0% USG with mid-single-digit volume growth. Hair Care led the segment with double-digit growth, driven by strong performance from Sunsilk and Dove.
*   **Personal Care Performance:** Reported 5.0% USG, with Skin Cleansing delivering high-single-digit growth supported by Lux and Dove.
*   **Margin Trends:** Operating EBITDA stood at ₹3,841 Crore, up 6.0% year-on-year. The EBITDA margin stood at 23.7%, representing a sequential improvement of 40 basis points, supported by stable material costs.
*   **Dividend:** The Board declared a final dividend of ₹22 per share for the fiscal year ended March 31, 2026.

## Management commentary
Rohit Jawa, CEO and Managing Director, shared his comments during the earnings conference:

"In Q4, HUL delivered a steady performance with consolidated turnover growth of 8% and a volume growth of 6%, which is our highest in the last 12 quarters. This volume recovery is an encouraging signal, showing that our efforts to drive market development and premiumization are yielding results. 

Our Home Care and Beauty & Wellbeing portfolios performed well, supported by double-digit growth in fabric wash and hair care. Following the demerger of our ice cream business, we have sharpened our focus on our core FMCG brands, helping to improve our operating efficiencies. While the pricing environment remains competitive, our EBITDA margin improved sequentially to 23.7%. We remain focused on volume-led growth and continuing to invest in our brands to drive long-term value."

## Future guidance
*   **Volume and Sales Targets:** The company expects volume growth to remain in the mid-single-digit range, supported by a gradual recovery in rural consumption.
*   **Margin Outlook:** HUL aims to maintain its operating EBITDA margins in the range of 23% to 24%, balancing input cost trends with investments in advertising and promotions.
*   **Portfolio Focus:** Following the spin-off of Kwality Wall's, the company plans to focus resources on its core high-growth categories, particularly premium beauty actives and liquid home care products.
*   **Capital Expenditure:** Plans for FY27 capital expenditure focus on localized supply chain technology, packaging sustainability, and digital commerce integration.
