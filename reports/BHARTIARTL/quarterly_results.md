## Revenue
For the fourth quarter (Q4) ended March 31, 2026, Bharti Airtel reported a consolidated revenue from operations of ₹55,383 Crore. This represents a 15.7% year-on-year growth compared to the ₹47,876 Crore reported in Q4 FY25, and a 2.6% sequential increase compared to Q3 FY26. 
*   **India Business Revenue:** Stood at ₹39,566 Crore, growing 7.7% year-on-year, driven by premiumization and steady additions in broadband and postpaid users.
*   **India Mobile Revenue:** Increased by 8.3% year-on-year, supported by steady smartphone user migration.

## PAT
The company reported a consolidated Net Profit After Tax (PAT) of ₹7,325 Crore for Q4 FY26, down 34% compared to the consolidated net profit of ₹11,021 Crore in the corresponding quarter of the previous fiscal year. This decline was primarily due to a high base in Q4 FY25, which benefited from a net tax gain of ₹2,892 Crore. On a sequential basis, consolidated net profit grew 10.4% from the ₹6,630.50 Crore reported in Q3 FY26.

## EPS
For the quarter ended March 31, 2026, Bharti Airtel’s Basic Earnings Per Share (EPS) stood at ₹12.53, while Diluted Normalized EPS reached ₹16.98 per share, compared to ₹11.32 in the preceding quarter.

## Key operational highlights
*   **ARPU Expansion:** Average Revenue Per User (ARPU) rose to ₹257, compared to ₹245 in Q4 FY25, supported by the transition of customers to higher-value postpaid plans and premium data packs.
*   **Substantial Customer Additions:** The company added 0.8 million postpaid customers during the quarter, bringing the total postpaid subscriber base to 29 million. Over the fiscal year, smartphone data users increased by 20 million to account for 80% of the total mobile base.
*   **Broadband Traction:** The Homes segment recorded net additions of 1,135K subscribers sequentially, driven by the expansion of FTTH and fixed wireless broadband.
*   **Infrastructure Rollout:** During the quarter, Airtel deployed 2,426 telecom towers and 16,746 mobile broadband base stations to improve 5G density.
*   **EBITDA Margins:** Consolidated EBITDA rose 16.9% year-on-year to ₹32,038 Crore, reflecting an EBITDA margin of 57.8%. India operations maintained a strong EBITDA margin of 60.6%.

## Management commentary
Gopal Vittal, Executive Vice Chairman, shared the following during the Q4 FY26 earnings call:

"We ended the financial year on a strong note, demonstrating the resilience of our diversified portfolio across both India and Africa. FY26 has been marked by several key operational milestones, including crossing the 650 million global customer mark, launching telco-grade sovereign cloud solutions, and expanding our corporate data center network. 

Our focus on premiumization has supported our results, with our postpaid base reaching 29 million and our mobile ARPU rising to ₹257. While we continue to invest in expanding our 5G and fiber footprint, our disciplined capital management has allowed us to strengthen our balance sheet, which is reflected in our improving debt profiles. Our objective remains clear: to deliver quality connectivity and services while managing cost structures and operational efficiencies."

## Future guidance
*   **ARPU Targets:** The management team remains focused on its long-term target ARPU of ₹300, which it plans to achieve through continued tier-upgrades, digital service cross-selling, and selective pricing adjustments.
*   **Capital Expenditure:** Annual capital expenditure is expected to remain stable to support 5G densification, fiber backhaul expansion, and data center developments, while managing free cash generation.
*   **Africa Integration:** Following the promoter stake increase in Airtel Africa to 79%, the company expects a stronger consolidated contribution from Africa, targeting double-digit constant-currency growth across those operations.
*   **Operational Sustainability:** In partnership with Indus Towers, the company is prioritizing green energy, aiming to reduce diesel dependency across its network infrastructure by expanding battery and solar storage facilities over the next two years.
