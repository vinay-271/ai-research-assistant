## Company overview
Larsen & Toubro Limited (L&T) is India's largest technology, engineering, construction, manufacturing, and financial services conglomerate. Founded in 1938 by Danish engineers Henning Holck-Larsen and Søren Kristian Toubro, the company is headquartered in Mumbai, Maharashtra. L&T operates globally, with a strong footprint in West Asia (Middle East), Southeast Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The group is led by Chairman and Managing Director S. N. Subrahmanyan. L&T is a major player in driving India's core infrastructure, heavy engineering, and defense industrialization, while steadily diversifying into high-tech domains like green hydrogen, semiconductors, data centers, and digital services.

## Business segments
*   **Infrastructure Projects:** The largest segment by revenue and order book size, covering heavy civil infrastructure, transport (roads, railways, metro rail), water systems, power transmission & distribution, and building & factories.
*   **Energy Projects:** Encompasses hydrocarbon engineering (offshore and onshore oil & gas projects), thermal and gas-based power plant EPC, and clean energy (solar, wind, and green hydrogen systems).
*   **Hi-Tech Manufacturing:** Focuses on heavy engineering (process plant equipment, nuclear piping), defense engineering (naval systems, armored vehicles, missile launchers), and aerospace assemblies.
*   **IT & Technology Services:** Comprises listed software and engineering subsidiaries, including LTIMindtree (IT services) and L&T Technology Services (LTTS - engineering R&D).
*   **Financial Services:** Operated through L&T Finance (formerly LTFS), focusing on retail lending, rural finance, and housing loans.
*   **Development Projects & Realty:** Includes L&T Realty (residential and commercial developments) and concessions such as the Hyderabad Metro Rail.

## Products
*   **Engineering & Construction (EPC) Solutions:** Turnkey construction of airports, high-speed rail corridors, marine terminals, nuclear power plants, and oil refineries.
*   **Defense & Aerospace Systems:** Tactical military communication gear, land weapon systems (K9 Vajra-T), naval warships, submarines, and rocket motor casings.
*   **Heavy Process Equipment:** Reactor vessels, high-pressure heat exchangers, and steam generators for fertilizer, petrochemical, and power plants.
*   **Financial Products:** Two-wheeler loans, personal loans, home loans, farm equipment loans, and micro-business credit.
*   **Digital and Engineering Services:** Semiconductor design, physical AI models, industrial digital twin software (NVIDIA Omniverse integrated platforms), and enterprise cloud consulting.

## Financial highlights
Consolidated financial parameters for the full financial year ended March 31, 2026, show steady operating metrics:
*   **Consolidated Revenue from Operations:** Reached ₹2,85,874 Crore, marking a 12% year-on-year growth compared to ₹2,55,734 Crore in FY25.
*   **Consolidated Net Profit (PAT):** Reported at ₹16,084 Crore, a 7% increase compared to ₹15,037 Crore in the previous fiscal year. 
*   **Consolidated Recurring PAT:** Grew 18% year-on-year to ₹17,238 Crore, indicating strong operational profitability.
*   **Annual Group Order Inflows:** Reached a record ₹4,35,590 Crore, registering a year-on-year growth of 22%, which significantly exceeded the initial management guidance of 10%.
*   **Consolidated Order Book:** Stood at an all-time high of ₹7,40,327 Crore as of March 31, 2026, providing long-term revenue visibility.
*   **Fitch Credit Rating:** Affirmed at 'BBB+' with a stable outlook in June 2026, supported by robust cash flows and leverage headroom.

## Competitive advantages
*   **Execution and Engineering Capabilities:** Unmatched capability in executing ultra-mega EPC projects on schedule and with high quality within the Indian subcontinent and West Asia.
*   **Diversified Order Book:** A balanced order book consisting of 48% domestic and 52% international projects, which provides a natural hedge against localized economic slow-downs.
*   **Prudent Capital Allocation:** The systematic exit from capital-intensive non-core businesses (such as Nabha Power and Hyderabad Metro concession reduction) to redeploy capital into high-margin tech-led segments.
*   **Strong Balance Sheet Liquidity:** Healthy free cash generation and a net cash position in the parent company, allowing the group to fund new growth areas like semiconductor design and green hydrogen plants.

## Risk factors
*   **Geopolitical Conflict in West Asia:** With international orders making up 52% of the consolidated order book—heavily concentrated in West Asia—any escalation in regional tensions presents supply chain, cost inflation, and execution risks.
*   **Legacy Margin Pressures:** Cost inflation in older, fixed-price hydrocarbon and infrastructure contracts has led to margin compression in the Energy Projects segment.
*   **Domestic Manpower Bottlenecks:** Execution speed on large-scale civil projects continues to face challenges from skilled labor shortages and changing labor regulatory frameworks.
*   **Commodity Price Fluctuation:** Price swings in input materials like structural steel, copper, and cement directly impact operating margins on long-cycle construction jobs.

## Management outlook
L&T is entering its new five-year strategic planning cycle, **Lakshya '31**, succeeding the Lakshya '26 plan. Under Chairman S. N. Subrahmanyan, the company's next phase is focused on shifting from traditional EPC execution toward technology-led and green-energy manufacturing. L&T is investing in advanced fields like AI-driven digital twin platforms, green hydrogen electrolyzer plants, commercial data centers, and fabless semiconductor designs. The company expects to maintain double-digit revenue growth in the range of 10% to 12% for the upcoming fiscal year, while working to improve operating margins as older legacy projects are completed.
